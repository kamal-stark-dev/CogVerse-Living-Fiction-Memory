Loki Laufeyson is the Asgardian god of mischief, adopted son of Odin, and one of the most complex characters in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Loki begins as a prince who feels inferior to Thor and later becomes an antagonist driven by resentment, ambition, and identity crisis. Loki's early life is shaped by the revelation that he is the son of Laufey rather than Odin, and that truth intensifies his need to prove himself.

Loki is closely connected to Thor, Odin, Frigga, and later Sylvie and the TVA. Loki invades Earth using the Tesseract, leads the Chitauri attack on New York, and repeatedly clashes with the Avengers. Even when Loki acts as an enemy, the character retains moments of humor, vulnerability, and surprising loyalty.

Loki's arc changes dramatically after his death and continued survival across alternate timelines. The Loki who escapes with the Tesseract becomes entangled with the TVA and begins a new journey centered on self-understanding rather than conquest. Loki evolves from a would-be conqueror into someone who can think beyond himself, making him one of the MCU's strongest examples of redemption through growth.
