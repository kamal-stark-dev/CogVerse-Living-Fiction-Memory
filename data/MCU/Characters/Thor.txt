Thor Odinson is the Asgardian god of thunder and the prince, later king, of Asgard. Thor begins as a proud and impulsive warrior whose arrogance causes conflict with Odin and leads to his exile on Earth. During his time among humans, Thor learns humility, compassion, and restraint, and he becomes worthy of Mjolnir again after proving his growth.

Thor is strongly connected to Loki, Odin, Frigga, and Jane Foster. His relationship with Loki is especially complex because Thor sees Loki as a brother even when Loki becomes an enemy. Thor later joins the Avengers and becomes one of their most powerful members. Thor fights in major conflicts including the Battle of New York, the battle against Malekith, the destruction of Asgard, the war with Thanos, and the final battle in Endgame.

Thor's character development centers on responsibility and grief. He repeatedly loses his home, his family, and his people, yet he keeps returning to battle because he believes Asgardian power should protect others rather than dominate them. Thor eventually wields Stormbreaker after Mjolnir is destroyed and becomes one of the few heroes capable of challenging Thanos directly.

Thor's later life is marked by loss, depression, recovery, and renewed purpose. After the Blip and the events of Endgame, Thor chooses to leave New Asgard with the Guardians of the Galaxy, reflecting his continuing search for identity beyond kingship. Thor remains one of the most important gods and warriors in the MCU.
