Infinity War is the broad conflict in which Thanos pursued the Infinity Stones across the MCU and clashed with the Avengers, the Guardians of the Galaxy, and their allies. The event includes the battles on Titan, in Wakanda, and across multiple locations where different teams tried to stop Thanos from completing his plan. Infinity War is the turning point that brings the Infinity Saga into its darkest phase.

The main participants included Thanos, the Black Order, the Avengers, the Guardians of the Galaxy, Doctor Strange, Iron Man, Spider-Man, Thor, and the Wakandan defenders. The event linked earlier MCU stories into a single cosmic crisis and showed that the heroes were fighting a foe who had prepared far in advance. It also revealed the cost of disunity because the heroes were often separated when they needed to act together.

The immediate consequence of Infinity War was the Snap and the death of half of all life in the universe. The long-term consequence was the Blip era and the desperate search for a way to undo Thanos's victory. Infinity War remains the MCU's clearest example of a story that escalates across multiple worlds and ends in catastrophic defeat.
