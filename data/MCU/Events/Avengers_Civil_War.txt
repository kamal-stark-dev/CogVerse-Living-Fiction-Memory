The Avengers Civil War was the internal conflict that split the Avengers into opposing factions after disagreement over the Sokovia Accords and the handling of Bucky Barnes. The event occurred when Tony Stark and Steve Rogers could no longer agree on how the Avengers should respond to government oversight and personal loyalty. The conflict turned former allies into enemies and deeply damaged the unity of the superhero team.

The main factions were led by Tony Stark and Steve Rogers, with Wanda Maximoff, Natasha Romanoff, Clint Barton, Vision, Black Panther, and others drawn into the dispute. The fight became personal as the issue of accountability collided with the revelation of Bucky Barnes's role in the death of Howard and Maria Stark. The conflict showed that moral disagreement among heroes can be as destructive as external warfare.

The immediate consequence of the Civil War was the fracture of the Avengers and the weakening of Earth's defense network. The long-term consequence was that the team was divided when Thanos arrived, making it harder to respond to the larger cosmic threat. The event remains one of the MCU's clearest examples of how ideology can break even the strongest alliances.
