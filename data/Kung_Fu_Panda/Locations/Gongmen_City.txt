Gongmen City is a large coastal metropolis located somewhere along the southern coast of China. The city appears only in the events of *Kung Fu Panda 2* and serves as one of the most politically and strategically important locations within the story. Gongmen City is characterized by its dense urban design, layered construction, bustling commercial activity, and extremely large population. The city contains stacked buildings, narrow interconnected alleyways, countless doorways, and multiple elevation levels, creating a complex environment that can easily confuse unfamiliar visitors. The surrounding landscape consists of rocky terrain covered with lush vegetation, while the city itself is connected to the sea through a large harbor that supports maritime travel and commerce.

For many generations, Gongmen City was ruled by the Peacock royal family. Under the leadership of the peacock dynasty, Gongmen City became one of the most prosperous cities in China. The Peacock family invented fireworks, an achievement that brought joy, celebration, technological advancement, and economic prosperity to the city's inhabitants. Fireworks became closely associated with Gongmen City's cultural identity and represented the city's greatest technological innovation. The prosperity created by the Peacock family established Gongmen City as an influential political, economic, and cultural center.

The ancestral palace of the Peacock family served as the political center of Gongmen City during the rule of the peacock dynasty. The palace symbolized the authority and legitimacy of the ruling family. Other important locations within the city included Gongmen Jail, which had originally served as a kung fu training school before becoming a prison, the Fireworks Factory where fireworks were manufactured, and the city harbor that connected Gongmen City with the rest of China through maritime trade and transportation. The Tower of the Sacred Flame stood as another major landmark and later became the location of a significant battle during Lord Shen's return.

The city's history changed dramatically after Shen, the only heir of the Peacock family, began pursuing the militarization of fireworks. Rather than preserving fireworks as instruments of celebration, Shen sought to transform them into weapons of war. Concerned by Shen's growing obsession, Shen's parents consulted the royal court's Soothsayer. The Soothsayer foresaw that if Shen continued along his current path, he would eventually be defeated by a warrior of black and white. Shen overheard this prophecy and interpreted it as referring to the giant pandas living in the nearby Panda Village.

Believing that eliminating the pandas would prevent the prophecy from coming true, Shen organized an army of wolves and launched an attack against Panda Village. The massacre resulted in the destruction of the panda community and permanently altered the lives of many survivors, including Po and his family. Although Shen believed his actions would secure his future, the massacre instead fulfilled the chain of events that would eventually lead to his downfall.

When Shen returned to Gongmen City after the massacre, his parents condemned his actions. Horrified that their son had committed genocide in pursuit of power, the Peacock rulers stripped Shen of his birthright, banished him from Gongmen City, and ordered him never to return. Shen interpreted his exile as betrayal and vowed revenge against both his family and all of China. This decision marked the end of the Peacock dynasty's direct succession.

Sometime after Shen's exile, Shen's parents died while grieving the loss of their son and the consequences of their decision. Because the Peacock family no longer had a legitimate heir, political authority over Gongmen City transferred to the Kung Fu Council. The Kung Fu Council became the governing body responsible for protecting the city and preserving peace. Leadership of the council consisted of Master Thundering Rhino, Master Storming Ox, and Master Croc. Under their stewardship, Gongmen City remained peaceful for more than twenty years.

More than two decades after his banishment, Shen returned to reclaim his ancestral home. By this time, Shen had successfully completed his research into weaponizing fireworks and had developed powerful cannons capable of defeating even the greatest kung fu masters. When Shen confronted the Kung Fu Council, Master Thundering Rhino refused to surrender and challenged Shen directly. Shen demonstrated the destructive power of his new weapon by killing Master Thundering Rhino with a cannon, proving that traditional kung fu alone could no longer stop him.

Following Thundering Rhino's death, Shen threatened to bombard Gongmen City if Master Storming Ox and Master Croc continued resisting. In order to protect the city's civilian population from mass destruction, both masters surrendered. Their surrender allowed Shen to seize political and military control of Gongmen City with very little additional resistance.

After the conquest, Gongmen City entered a period of military occupation. Shen established an authoritarian regime supported by his wolf army. The wolves patrolled the streets, intimidated civilians, enforced Shen's authority, and confiscated metal from throughout the city. The collected metal was transported to the Fireworks Factory, where it was used to manufacture additional cannons. The city's economy and industrial capacity were redirected toward military production rather than peaceful prosperity. Gongmen City's transformation from a flourishing commercial center into the headquarters of Shen's military campaign represented one of the greatest political changes in its history.

News of Gongmen City's occupation eventually reached the Valley of Peace. In response, Shifu dispatched Po, the Dragon Warrior, together with the Furious Five to liberate the city and stop Shen before he launched a campaign to conquer all of China. Gongmen City consequently became the primary battlefield for the conflict between Shen's expanding empire and China's greatest kung fu defenders.

Upon entering Gongmen City, Po and the Furious Five attempted to infiltrate the occupied metropolis but were quickly discovered by Shen's wolves. Despite constant pursuit through the city's maze-like streets and alleyways, they successfully rescued a sheep from wolf harassment. The grateful sheep informed them that Master Storming Ox and Master Croc were imprisoned inside Gongmen Jail.

Po and the Furious Five infiltrated Gongmen Jail and located both imprisoned masters. However, Storming Ox and Croc refused to escape because Shen had threatened to destroy Gongmen City with his cannons if they resisted. Their refusal demonstrated the extent to which Shen's military technology had undermined the traditional principles of kung fu and forced respected masters into reluctant submission.

After another confrontation with Shen's forces, Po and the Furious Five were captured and transported to the Tower of the Sacred Flame. A battle inside the tower resulted in significant structural damage and ultimately destroyed the landmark. Angered by Po's escape, Shen ordered every wolf stationed throughout Gongmen City to assemble at the Fireworks Factory in preparation for his planned conquest of China.

Believing that Po's traumatic memories endangered the mission, Tigress ordered Po to remain inside Gongmen Jail while the Furious Five infiltrated the Fireworks Factory. Po ignored the order and entered the factory independently. During the battle inside the factory, Shen manipulated Po emotionally by falsely claiming that Po's parents had abandoned him. This psychological attack distracted Po long enough for Shen to strike him with his largest cannon, leading Shen to believe that the Dragon Warrior had been killed.

Confident that Po was dead, Shen assembled his fleet and army within Gongmen City and prepared to launch a full-scale invasion of China. However, Po survived the cannon blast after achieving inner peace and returned to confront Shen. Shifu, the Furious Five, Master Storming Ox, and Master Croc joined the final battle. The conflict spread throughout Gongmen City's harbor and surrounding waters as Shen's naval fleet engaged the kung fu masters.

The final confrontation ended with the destruction of Shen's army and Shen's death. With Shen defeated, the military occupation of Gongmen City came to an end. The surviving wolves no longer controlled the city, the citizens were liberated, and the immediate threat of conquest disappeared. Fireworks launched from Shen's flagship illuminated the sky above Gongmen City, symbolically transforming the city's greatest technological invention from an instrument of war back into a celebration of peace and freedom.

Throughout its history, Gongmen City experienced several distinct political eras. It began as the capital of the Peacock dynasty, transitioned into governance by the Kung Fu Council following the extinction of the royal succession, briefly became an authoritarian military state under Lord Shen, and was ultimately liberated by Po and his allies. Each transition reflected major shifts in leadership, ideology, and the balance between peace and military power.

Gongmen City maintains strong connections with several important locations throughout the Kung Fu Panda universe. The city is closely connected to the nearby Panda Village because Shen's fear of the prophecy led directly to the massacre that occurred there. Gongmen City is politically connected to the Valley of Peace because the fall of Gongmen prompted Shifu to dispatch Po and the Furious Five to intervene. The city's harbor connects it with the wider regions of China and enabled Shen's planned naval conquest.

Several organizations have exercised authority within Gongmen City during different periods. The Peacock royal family ruled the city for generations. The Kung Fu Council governed and protected the city following Shen's exile. Shen's wolf army served as the city's occupying military force after his conquest. The Furious Five and other kung fu masters later participated in the city's liberation, although they never became its permanent rulers.

Important residents of Gongmen City include the Soothsayer, Master Storming Ox, Master Croc, and numerous unnamed civilians. Important visitors include Po, the Furious Five, Shifu, and the Four Constellations. Lord Shen was both a former resident and later an invading ruler who returned to reclaim his birthplace.

Gongmen City remains one of the most historically significant locations in the Kung Fu Panda universe because it is the birthplace of fireworks, the ancestral home of the Peacock family, the origin of Shen's rise to power, the location where cannon technology challenged the supremacy of kung fu, and the setting for the final conflict that ended Shen's campaign of conquest. The city's history demonstrates how innovation can be used either to create prosperity or to wage war, making Gongmen City a central symbol of both civilization and the consequences of corrupt ambition.
