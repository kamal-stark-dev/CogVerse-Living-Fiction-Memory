The central event chronicles Harry Potter's fourth year at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry during the 1994–1995 academic year. The event is centered on the revival of the Triwizard Tournament, the conspiracy orchestrated by Barty Crouch Jr. while impersonating Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody, Harry Potter's forced participation in the tournament, the rebirth of Lord Voldemort, the murder of Cedric Diggory, and the public beginning of the Second Wizarding War. The event transforms the political and social landscape of the wizarding world by ending the period during which Lord Voldemort was believed to be powerless and exposing deep divisions between those who accept his return and those who deny it.

The event begins during the early morning of 16 August 1994 in the village of Little Hangleton. Frank Bryce, an elderly caretaker, enters the abandoned Riddle House after believing intruders have entered the property. Frank Bryce overhears Lord Voldemort planning to capture Harry Potter with the assistance of Peter Pettigrew, also known as Wormtail, and an unidentified young wizard. Lord Voldemort murders Frank Bryce with the Killing Curse to eliminate the witness. This murder demonstrates that Lord Voldemort has regained enough magical power to kill while still lacking a physical body. Frank Bryce's death also becomes one of the magical echoes later produced during Priori Incantatem.

Harry Potter experiences this murder through a vivid dream connected to his magical link with Lord Voldemort. Harry Potter awakens disturbed by the vision. The dream establishes that Harry Potter's scar continues to provide an involuntary connection between Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort. This connection becomes increasingly important throughout the event because it repeatedly exposes Harry Potter to Lord Voldemort's plans.

Soon afterward, Harry Potter travels with the Weasley family, Amos Diggory, and Cedric Diggory by Portkey to attend the 422nd Quidditch World Cup. Ireland defeats Bulgaria in the championship match. The tournament initially represents unity and celebration among the international wizarding community.

Following the Quidditch World Cup, masked Death Eaters attack the campsite, terrorizing witches and wizards. The attack demonstrates that many former followers of Lord Voldemort remain active despite his apparent disappearance. During the confusion, Harry Potter becomes separated from his companions and loses consciousness. After the attack, the Dark Mark appears in the sky. The Dark Mark is cast by the unidentified young wizard from Harry Potter's dream, who immediately escapes before being identified. The reappearance of the Dark Mark causes widespread fear because it is the symbol historically associated with Lord Voldemort and his Death Eaters. The incident marks the first major public indication that Lord Voldemort's influence is returning.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger return to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry for their fourth year. Albus Dumbledore announces that Hogwarts will host the revived Triwizard Tournament, an international magical competition involving Hogwarts, Beauxbatons Academy of Magic, and Durmstrang Institute. The revival of the tournament is intended to strengthen cooperation and friendship among European magical schools after many years without the competition.

Delegations from Beauxbatons Academy of Magic and Durmstrang Institute arrive at Hogwarts. Fleur Delacour represents Beauxbatons, while Viktor Krum, an internationally famous Quidditch player, represents Durmstrang. Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody is introduced as the new Defence Against the Dark Arts professor. Unknown to everyone except Lord Voldemort and his conspirators, the individual appearing as Alastor Moody is actually Barty Crouch Jr. disguised through continuous use of Polyjuice Potion. This deception becomes the foundation of the conspiracy that drives the entire school year.

The Goblet of Fire selects Cedric Diggory as Hogwarts' champion, Fleur Delacour as Beauxbatons' champion, and Viktor Krum as Durmstrang's champion. Unexpectedly, the Goblet of Fire ejects a fourth name and selects Harry Potter despite Harry Potter being underage and having never entered the competition. Albus Dumbledore immediately questions Harry Potter regarding the selection. Harry Potter truthfully denies entering his name. Barty Crouch Sr. explains that the magical contract created by the Goblet of Fire is binding, forcing Harry Potter to compete regardless of the circumstances.

Harry Potter's unexpected selection dramatically alters his relationships within Hogwarts. Many students believe Harry Potter cheated to gain additional fame and attention. Ron Weasley initially believes Harry Potter secretly entered the tournament without informing him. Ron Weasley becomes jealous and distances himself from Harry Potter. Harry Potter experiences increasing isolation because many classmates distrust him despite his innocence. Barty Crouch Jr., while disguised as Alastor Moody, publicly supports Harry Potter's innocence to ensure that Harry Potter remains in the tournament according to Lord Voldemort's plan.

Before the First Task, Rubeus Hagrid secretly shows Harry Potter the dragons that will guard the Golden Eggs. Harry Potter shares this information with Cedric Diggory because Cedric previously treated Harry Potter fairly despite the controversy surrounding the tournament. This exchange strengthens the mutual respect between Harry Potter and Cedric Diggory.

The First Task requires each champion to retrieve a Golden Egg protected by a dragon. Harry Potter successfully summons his Firebolt broomstick and outflies the dragon before retrieving the Golden Egg. Harry Potter's performance demonstrates courage, quick thinking, and exceptional flying ability. Barty Crouch Jr., disguised as Alastor Moody, quietly guides events so that Harry Potter succeeds because Harry Potter must survive until the final task.

After witnessing the danger Harry Potter willingly faced, Ron Weasley realizes Harry Potter would never voluntarily enter such a deadly competition merely for attention. Ron Weasley apologizes to Harry Potter, and their friendship is fully restored. This reconciliation repairs one of Harry Potter's most important personal relationships before the increasingly dangerous later stages of the tournament.

During the Christmas holidays, Hogwarts hosts the Yule Ball as part of the Triwizard Tournament traditions. The event strengthens relationships between students from different magical schools while also exposing emotional tensions. Ron Weasley becomes jealous after discovering that Hermione Granger attends the ball with Viktor Krum. This incident reveals the growing emotional complexity of Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger's friendship, although neither openly acknowledges romantic feelings.

Harry Potter experiences another vision connected to Lord Voldemort in which Harry witnesses Frank Bryce's murder from the victim's perspective. The repeated visions demonstrate that Lord Voldemort's growing strength is intensifying the magical connection between himself and Harry Potter.

Cedric Diggory repays Harry Potter's earlier kindness by providing a clue for solving the mystery of the Golden Egg. Cedric Diggory advises Harry Potter to open the egg while taking a bath. Harry Potter follows the advice in the Prefects' Bathroom and learns that the next challenge involves retrieving something precious from the Black Lake.

Before the Second Task, Neville Longbottom provides Harry Potter with Gillyweed after receiving information indirectly arranged by Barty Crouch Jr. Harry Potter consumes the Gillyweed, allowing him to breathe underwater for an extended period.

During the Second Task, Harry Potter dives into the Black Lake to rescue Ron Weasley, who has been magically placed beneath the water by the tournament organizers. Harry Potter also discovers Gabrielle Delacour, Fleur Delacour's younger sister, still trapped after Fleur Delacour is prevented from completing the task by Grindylows. Harry Potter refuses to abandon Gabrielle Delacour despite being told to rescue only Ron Weasley. Harry Potter's compassion and moral integrity cause him to rescue both hostages. Although Harry Potter finishes second behind Cedric Diggory, the judges reward Harry Potter for displaying exceptional moral character. Fleur Delacour becomes deeply grateful to Harry Potter for saving Gabrielle Delacour, establishing mutual respect between their families.

After the Second Task, Harry Potter briefly speaks with Barty Crouch Sr. Barty Crouch Sr. praises Harry Potter's accomplishments and states that James Potter and Lily Potter would have been proud of their son. The conversation is interrupted when Alastor Moody aggressively accuses Barty Crouch Sr. of attempting to influence Harry Potter. During the confrontation, Barty Crouch Sr. notices Alastor Moody's distinctive tongue movement and reacts with visible alarm. This subtle exchange foreshadows that Barty Crouch Sr. recognizes characteristics belonging to his son rather than the real Alastor Moody.

While walking through the Forbidden Forest with Rubeus Hagrid, Harry Potter discovers the murdered body of Barty Crouch Sr. The murder eliminates one of the few individuals capable of exposing Lord Voldemort's conspiracy. Harry Potter immediately informs Albus Dumbledore. Cornelius Fudge argues against cancelling the Triwizard Tournament because he refuses to appear fearful. Cornelius Fudge's decision demonstrates his growing tendency to prioritize political reputation over emerging evidence of danger.

While waiting in Albus Dumbledore's office, Harry Potter accidentally activates a hidden mechanism that reveals Albus Dumbledore's Pensieve. Harry Potter falls into one of Albus Dumbledore's stored memories and witnesses a Ministry of Magic courtroom trial. Harry Potter learns that Igor Karkaroff once served as a Death Eater. Harry Potter also learns that Barty Crouch Sr. had a son named Barty Crouch Jr., who was convicted of serving Lord Voldemort. Harry Potter recognizes that Barty Crouch Jr. resembles the unidentified young wizard from his recurring dreams. Although Harry Potter reports this realization to Albus Dumbledore, Albus Dumbledore advises Harry Potter to set the matter aside until more evidence emerges.

Harry Potter later observes Igor Karkaroff showing Severus Snape his Dark Mark. The Dark Mark has begun becoming increasingly visible because Lord Voldemort is regaining strength. Severus Snape also accuses Harry Potter of stealing Boomslang skin from his stores and suspects Polyjuice Potion is being brewed. Although Harry Potter is innocent, Severus Snape unknowingly identifies evidence connected to Barty Crouch Jr.'s ongoing impersonation of Alastor Moody.

The Third Task consists of navigating an enchanted maze containing magical obstacles while searching for the Triwizard Cup positioned at its center. The maze itself is enchanted to confuse, separate, and disorient the champions.

During the Third Task, Viktor Krum falls under magical influence and attacks Fleur Delacour and Cedric Diggory. Cedric Diggory successfully disarms Viktor Krum before Harry Potter prevents Cedric Diggory from retaliating further. Harry Potter and Cedric Diggory continue independently until Harry Potter rescues Cedric Diggory after magical roots begin pulling Cedric beneath the ground. Their repeated acts of mutual assistance establish complete trust between the two Hogwarts champions.

Harry Potter and Cedric Diggory reach the Triwizard Cup together. Rather than competing against one another, they agree to share the victory. When both champions touch the cup simultaneously, they discover that the Triwizard Cup has secretly been transformed into a Portkey by Barty Crouch Jr. The Portkey transports Harry Potter and Cedric Diggory to the graveyard at Little Hangleton. This transportation fulfills the central objective of Lord Voldemort's year-long conspiracy.

Immediately after arriving in Little Hangleton, Peter Pettigrew murders Cedric Diggory on Lord Voldemort's command using the Killing Curse. Cedric Diggory dies without understanding why he has been targeted. Cedric Diggory's murder represents the first death of one of Harry Potter's fellow students and marks the definitive end of Harry Potter's relatively protected childhood.

Peter Pettigrew binds Harry Potter to the graveyard monument before beginning a complex ritual to restore Lord Voldemort's body. Peter Pettigrew places the bone of Tom Riddle Sr., Lord Voldemort's father, into a magical cauldron. Peter Pettigrew voluntarily cuts off his own hand as the flesh willingly sacrificed by a servant. Peter Pettigrew then takes blood from Harry Potter as blood forcibly taken from an enemy. The ritual restores Lord Voldemort to a complete physical body. Lord Voldemort rewards Peter Pettigrew by magically creating a new silver hand to replace the sacrificed one.

After his resurrection, Lord Voldemort summons the Death Eaters to the graveyard. Lord Voldemort condemns many Death Eaters for abandoning him during his years of weakness while explaining how Harry Potter survived the failed Killing Curse years earlier. Lord Voldemort declares that Harry Potter's blood has enabled his rebirth and believes this achievement will eliminate the magical protection previously provided by Lily Potter's sacrifice.

Lord Voldemort releases Harry Potter from the monument to conduct a formal duel. Lord Voldemort first tortures Harry Potter using the Cruciatus Curse before forcing Harry Potter into magical combat. During the duel, the twin cores shared by Harry Potter's wand and Lord Voldemort's wand produce the rare magical phenomenon known as Priori Incantatem. Because both wands contain feathers from the same phoenix, the spells become magically connected.

Priori Incantatem causes echoes of the most recent victims killed by Lord Voldemort's wand to emerge. Frank Bryce, Cedric Diggory, James Potter, and Lily Potter appear as magical echoes. James Potter instructs Harry Potter to escape once the magical connection breaks. Cedric Diggory asks Harry Potter to return his body to his father. Lily Potter tells Harry Potter when to break the magical connection. The echoes temporarily distract Lord Voldemort and the assembled Death Eaters, allowing Harry Potter to summon the Triwizard Cup with the Summoning Charm and escape while carrying Cedric Diggory's body.

Harry Potter returns to Hogwarts with Cedric Diggory's corpse. The assembled students, teachers, and visiting delegations immediately realize that a tragedy has occurred. Harry Potter attempts to explain Lord Voldemort's return, but Alastor Moody secretly escorts Harry Potter to his office before Harry Potter can publicly reveal the truth.

Inside the office, the false Alastor Moody reveals that he orchestrated every significant event of the school year. Barty Crouch Jr. admits that he secretly entered Harry Potter into the Goblet of Fire, manipulated Harry Potter through each tournament task, encouraged Rubeus Hagrid to reveal the dragons, ensured Cedric Diggory provided clues, arranged for Neville Longbottom to supply Gillyweed, bewitched Viktor Krum during the Third Task, and transformed the Triwizard Cup into a Portkey. Barty Crouch Jr. explains that every action served the single purpose of delivering Harry Potter alive to Lord Voldemort.

Albus Dumbledore realizes that the real Alastor Moody would never separate Harry Potter from his protection. Albus Dumbledore arrives with Minerva McGonagall and Severus Snape before Barty Crouch Jr. can kill Harry Potter. After being disarmed, Barty Crouch Jr. is forced to drink Veritaserum. Under the effects of the truth potion, Barty Crouch Jr. reveals the existence of a magical trunk containing the imprisoned real Alastor Moody. The Polyjuice Potion wears off, exposing Barty Crouch Jr.'s true identity. The revelation explains every suspicious event that occurred throughout the school year and uncovers the conspiracy that successfully resurrected Lord Voldemort.

At the conclusion of the school year, Hogwarts holds a memorial feast honoring Cedric Diggory. Albus Dumbledore publicly announces that Cedric Diggory died because Lord Voldemort has returned. Albus Dumbledore directly contradicts Cornelius Fudge's attempts to suppress the truth. This declaration creates the first major political divide of the renewed conflict. Albus Dumbledore chooses transparency and preparation for war, while Cornelius Fudge chooses denial and preservation of public confidence.

The emotional consequences of the event permanently affect the principal characters. Harry Potter witnesses Cedric Diggory's murder, survives direct torture by Lord Voldemort, and becomes the first reliable witness to Lord Voldemort's resurrection. Cedric Diggory's death profoundly affects Harry Potter because Cedric Diggory treated Harry Potter with fairness and friendship throughout the tournament. Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger recognize that Harry Potter has experienced trauma unlike anything in previous years. Amos Diggory loses his son, creating one of the most tragic personal losses of the renewed war.

The political consequences reshape the wizarding world. Lord Voldemort regains a physical body and reunites his Death Eaters. Peter Pettigrew successfully restores his master and is rewarded for his loyalty. Barty Crouch Jr.'s infiltration demonstrates that even Hogwarts is vulnerable to deception. Cornelius Fudge's refusal to acknowledge Lord Voldemort's return creates institutional denial within the Ministry of Magic. Albus Dumbledore begins preparing for the coming conflict because he understands that open war has become inevitable.

The long-term consequences extend far beyond the Triwizard Tournament. The tournament itself becomes overshadowed by Cedric Diggory's death and Lord Voldemort's resurrection. Harry Potter's childhood effectively ends as he becomes an active participant in the renewed war against Lord Voldemort. The Death Eaters are reunited under their restored master, the Ministry of Magic begins a period of political denial, and the wizarding world enters the opening phase of the Second Wizarding War. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger leave Hogwarts fully aware that the safety and innocence of previous school years have been permanently lost and that the struggle against Lord Voldemort has entered a far more dangerous era.
