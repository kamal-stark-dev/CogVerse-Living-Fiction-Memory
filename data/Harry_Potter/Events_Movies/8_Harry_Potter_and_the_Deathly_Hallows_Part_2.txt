Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 depicts the concluding events of the Second Wizarding War and serves as the final chapter of Harry Potter's conflict with Lord Voldemort. The story begins immediately after the events of the previous installment, following Lord Voldemort's acquisition of the Elder Wand from Albus Dumbledore's tomb. Voldemort believes possession of the Elder Wand will guarantee his victory over Harry Potter, while Harry Potter and his allies continue the mission entrusted to them by Albus Dumbledore to locate and destroy Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes. The central conflict of the event is the race between Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort to determine whether Voldemort's immortality can be ended before he secures complete dominance over the wizarding world.

The event begins at Shell Cottage following the funeral of Dobby. Harry Potter mourns Dobby while examining the two-way mirror that previously allowed Aberforth Dumbledore to secretly watch over him. Harry Potter questions Griphook about Bellatrix Lestrange's vault at Gringotts Wizarding Bank. Griphook explains that Bellatrix unknowingly possesses a counterfeit Sword of Gryffindor inside her vault, indicating that the genuine sword had already been removed. Harry Potter correctly concludes that Bellatrix Lestrange's extreme fear regarding her vault was caused by the presence of one of Lord Voldemort's Horcruxes. Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, and Ron Weasley negotiate with Griphook. Griphook agrees to guide them into Bellatrix Lestrange's vault in exchange for permanent ownership of the Sword of Gryffindor. The agreement demonstrates the increasing desperation of Harry Potter's mission because the Sword of Gryffindor is the group's most reliable weapon for destroying Horcruxes.

Harry Potter consults Garrick Ollivander regarding the stolen wands taken from Bellatrix Lestrange and Draco Malfoy. Garrick Ollivander identifies Bellatrix Lestrange's wand as unusually unyielding and confirms that Draco Malfoy's wand has transferred its allegiance to Harry Potter because Harry Potter defeated Draco Malfoy. Garrick Ollivander also explains his limited knowledge of the Deathly Hallows and warns Harry Potter that Voldemort's possession of the Elder Wand makes the Dark Lord appear nearly unbeatable. This conversation establishes the importance of wand allegiance, which later determines the outcome of the war.

Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, Ron Weasley, and Griphook infiltrate Gringotts Wizarding Bank. Hermione Granger disguises herself as Bellatrix Lestrange, Ron Weasley disguises himself as a foreign wizard, and Harry Potter remains hidden beneath the Invisibility Cloak. During the infiltration, Harry Potter and Ron Weasley use the Imperius Curse on the goblin Bogrod after Hermione Granger's disguise begins to fail. The group reaches Bellatrix Lestrange's vault but activates the Thief's Downfall, which removes magical disguises. Inside the vault, Harry Potter discovers Helga Hufflepuff's Cup, confirming it is one of Voldemort's Horcruxes. The Gemino Curse causes every treasure touched to multiply uncontrollably, nearly burying the group beneath endless gold.

Griphook immediately betrays Harry Potter after securing possession of the Sword of Gryffindor. Griphook escapes the vault while summoning Gringotts security. Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, and Ron Weasley survive by freeing the imprisoned Gringotts dragon and escaping on its back. During the escape, the dragon destroys portions of the bank while numerous goblins, including Bogrod, are killed. The successful theft of Helga Hufflepuff's Cup removes another layer of Voldemort's immortality but also alerts Voldemort that his Horcruxes are being systematically destroyed.

Harry Potter experiences another vision through his mental connection with Lord Voldemort. Voldemort murders Griphook and multiple Gringotts employees for failing to protect the Horcrux. Voldemort realizes that his enemies understand the existence of the Horcruxes and immediately concludes that the final hidden Horcrux at Hogwarts is endangered. Voldemort summons the Death Eaters and orders a full-scale assault on Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry Potter simultaneously realizes that Voldemort's emotional instability increases each time a Horcrux is destroyed, demonstrating that Voldemort becomes psychologically weaker as well as physically vulnerable.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger Apparate into Hogsmeade but activate magical Caterwauling alarms established by the Death Eaters. Aberforth Dumbledore rescues them and brings them into his home. Harry Potter learns that Aberforth Dumbledore secretly observed and protected the trio through the fragment of Sirius Black's two-way mirror and that Aberforth Dumbledore sent Dobby to rescue them from Malfoy Manor. Aberforth Dumbledore urges Harry Potter to abandon the mission because he believes Albus Dumbledore assigned Harry Potter an impossible suicide mission. Harry Potter refuses to surrender, expressing complete faith in Albus Dumbledore's judgment despite not fully understanding Dumbledore's intentions.

Aberforth Dumbledore summons Neville Longbottom through the hidden passage concealed behind Ariana Dumbledore's portrait. Neville Longbottom reveals that Hogwarts has become an oppressive institution under Severus Snape's leadership as Headmaster, with the Carrows abusing students while Dumbledore's Army secretly resists their rule from the Room of Requirement. Harry Potter receives overwhelming support from the hidden students, strengthening morale before the coming battle. Luna Lovegood suggests that the final Hogwarts Horcrux may be Rowena Ravenclaw's lost Diadem, beginning the search for the final hidden fragment of Voldemort's soul within the castle.

Harry Potter confronts Severus Snape inside the Great Hall before the assembled Hogwarts community. Harry Potter publicly accuses Severus Snape of murdering Albus Dumbledore. Minerva McGonagall immediately defends Harry Potter and duels Severus Snape. Severus Snape secretly redirects Minerva McGonagall's attacks toward the Carrows before escaping the castle. Lord Voldemort then broadcasts a magical message throughout Hogwarts demanding Harry Potter's surrender in exchange for the lives of everyone inside the castle. Pansy Parkinson urges students to capture Harry Potter, but the overwhelming majority of students, Dumbledore's Army, and the Order of the Phoenix stand beside Harry Potter instead. This moment confirms that Hogwarts collectively rejects Voldemort's rule.

Minerva McGonagall organizes Hogwarts' defense. She animates the castle's stone guardians to fight alongside the defenders while Filius Flitwick, Horace Slughorn, Molly Weasley, and other professors establish magical protective shields around Hogwarts. The defenders transform Hogwarts from a school into a fortress prepared for open warfare.

Harry Potter seeks Helena Ravenclaw, also known as the Grey Lady. Helena Ravenclaw initially refuses to reveal the location of Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem because Lord Voldemort previously corrupted it with Dark Magic. Harry Potter convinces Helena Ravenclaw that he intends only to destroy the artifact. Helena Ravenclaw reveals that Tom Riddle hid the diadem inside the Room of Requirement in the hidden chamber where lost objects are concealed. This revelation provides Harry Potter with the location of the final accessible Horcrux.

Simultaneously, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger enter the Chamber of Secrets to obtain basilisk fangs capable of destroying Horcruxes. Hermione Granger destroys Helga Hufflepuff's Cup with a basilisk fang. The destruction of the Horcrux generates magical shockwaves felt simultaneously by Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort. Immediately afterward, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger finally acknowledge their long-suppressed romantic feelings by sharing their first kiss, marking the culmination of years of emotional development.

Lord Voldemort personally destroys Hogwarts' protective shield, officially beginning the Battle of Hogwarts. The effort slightly damages the Elder Wand, foreshadowing its rejection of Voldemort's authority. Death Eaters, Dementors, giants, Acromantulas, and Snatchers invade Hogwarts while professors, students, members of the Order of the Phoenix, and Dumbledore's Army unite in defense of the castle. Neville Longbottom destroys the wooden bridge to prevent invading Snatchers from entering Hogwarts, killing Scabior and numerous attackers while surviving himself.

Harry Potter enters the Room of Requirement and locates Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem. Draco Malfoy, Gregory Goyle, and Blaise Zabini confront him. Harry Potter questions Draco Malfoy about Draco's earlier reluctance to identify Harry Potter at Malfoy Manor, exposing Draco Malfoy's growing moral conflict. Gregory Goyle recklessly casts Fiendfyre. Gregory Goyle cannot control the cursed fire, causing it to consume the Room of Requirement. Gregory Goyle dies after falling into the Fiendfyre. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger rescue both Draco Malfoy and Blaise Zabini despite their previous hostility, demonstrating Harry Potter's refusal to abandon human compassion even toward former enemies. Harry Potter destroys Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem with a basilisk fang before the Room of Requirement collapses in magical flames. Voldemort senses yet another Horcrux being destroyed and becomes increasingly unstable.

Harry Potter receives another vision showing Lord Voldemort summoning Severus Snape to the Boathouse. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger witness Voldemort explaining that he believes Severus Snape is the Elder Wand's true master because Snape killed Albus Dumbledore. Voldemort murders Severus Snape using Sectumsempra and orders Nagini to finish him. Voldemort believes Snape's death will transfer the Elder Wand's allegiance. Before dying, Severus Snape gives Harry Potter his memories and asks Harry Potter to look into his eyes because Harry Potter possesses Lily Evans Potter's eyes. These memories become the key to understanding the entire hidden history of the war.

The Battle of Hogwarts temporarily pauses while both sides recover their dead. Fred Weasley, Remus Lupin, Nymphadora Tonks, Lavender Brown, and numerous unnamed defenders are confirmed dead. The deaths of beloved members of the Order of the Phoenix and the Weasley family demonstrate the devastating human cost of resisting Voldemort's tyranny.

Harry Potter enters Albus Dumbledore's office and views Severus Snape's memories inside the Pensieve. Harry Potter learns that Severus Snape and Lily Evans were childhood friends before attending Hogwarts. Harry Potter witnesses James Potter and Sirius Black bullying Severus Snape during their school years. Harry Potter learns that Severus Snape loved Lily Evans throughout his life and unintentionally caused her death after revealing part of Sybill Trelawney's prophecy to Lord Voldemort. Overcome by guilt, Severus Snape defected from the Death Eaters and became Albus Dumbledore's most trusted agent.

The Pensieve memories reveal that Severus Snape secretly protected Harry Potter for many years while maintaining Voldemort's trust through Occlumency. Harry Potter learns that Severus Snape delayed the curse from Marvolo Gaunt's Ring and later agreed to kill Albus Dumbledore because Dumbledore was already dying and wished to spare Draco Malfoy from becoming a murderer. The memories further reveal that Harry Potter himself became an unintended Horcrux when Voldemort's Killing Curse rebounded during Harry Potter's infancy. Albus Dumbledore deliberately concealed this truth because Harry Potter would eventually need to sacrifice himself so that Voldemort's soul fragment inside Harry Potter could be destroyed. Harry Potter also learns that Severus Snape's silver doe Patronus matched Lily Evans Potter's Patronus, proving that Severus Snape remained loyal to Lily's memory throughout his entire life.

Harry Potter accepts that his own death is necessary to defeat Lord Voldemort. Harry Potter bids farewell to Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger before entering the Forbidden Forest alone. Harry Potter opens the Golden Snitch inscribed with "I open at the close" and retrieves the Resurrection Stone. Harry Potter summons the spirits of James Potter, Lily Potter, Sirius Black, and Remus Lupin. The four deceased loved ones accompany Harry Potter emotionally as he walks toward his willing sacrifice. Harry Potter abandons the Resurrection Stone in the forest so that its power cannot tempt future generations.

Lord Voldemort confronts Harry Potter inside the Forbidden Forest while Rubeus Hagrid remains imprisoned. Harry Potter willingly offers no resistance. Voldemort casts the Killing Curse using the Elder Wand. The spell destroys only the Horcrux fragment inside Harry Potter because Voldemort previously used Harry Potter's blood during his resurrection, unintentionally tethering Harry Potter's life to Voldemort's own survival.

Harry Potter awakens within a spiritual version of King's Cross Station where he meets Albus Dumbledore. Harry Potter observes the mutilated, helpless fragment of Voldemort's soul, symbolizing the permanently damaged condition of Voldemort's existence. Albus Dumbledore explains that Harry Potter survived because only the Horcrux inside him was destroyed. Albus Dumbledore allows Harry Potter to choose between remaining dead or returning to complete Voldemort's defeat. Harry Potter freely chooses to return, completing his transformation from reluctant hero into willing self-sacrificing leader.

Harry Potter pretends to remain dead after returning to consciousness. Narcissa Malfoy secretly discovers Harry Potter is alive while checking his body. Narcissa Malfoy asks whether Draco Malfoy survived the battle. Harry Potter silently confirms that Draco Malfoy lives. Narcissa Malfoy lies to Lord Voldemort by falsely declaring Harry Potter dead because her priority becomes reuniting with her son rather than serving Voldemort. Narcissa Malfoy's deception directly enables Voldemort's eventual defeat.

Lord Voldemort returns to Hogwarts carrying Harry Potter's apparently lifeless body. Voldemort announces victory and demands the defenders surrender. Neville Longbottom refuses despite Harry Potter's apparent death. Neville Longbottom declares that those who died sacrificed themselves for freedom and justice, inspiring renewed resistance. Neville Longbottom pulls the Sword of Gryffindor from the Sorting Hat, proving himself worthy of Gryffindor's ideals. Harry Potter simultaneously reveals himself alive, shattering Voldemort's psychological advantage and restoring hope to the defenders.

Many Death Eaters lose faith in Lord Voldemort after witnessing Harry Potter survive once again. Narcissa Malfoy, Draco Malfoy, and Lucius Malfoy abandon Voldemort entirely. Bellatrix Lestrange remains fanatically loyal and nearly murders Ginny Weasley. Molly Weasley intervenes to defend her daughter. Molly Weasley defeats and kills Bellatrix Lestrange after an intense duel, permanently eliminating Voldemort's most dangerous lieutenant.

Harry Potter confronts Lord Voldemort while Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, and Neville Longbottom focus on destroying Nagini. Harry Potter explains that Voldemort misunderstood Elder Wand loyalty. Severus Snape never became the Elder Wand's true master because Albus Dumbledore willingly accepted death. Draco Malfoy became the Elder Wand's master after disarming Albus Dumbledore atop the Astronomy Tower. Harry Potter later defeated Draco Malfoy and won Draco Malfoy's wand, unknowingly inheriting the Elder Wand's allegiance. Voldemort therefore never truly possessed mastery over the Elder Wand despite physically holding it.

Nagini attacks Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, but Neville Longbottom intervenes and decapitates Nagini with the Sword of Gryffindor. Nagini's destruction eliminates Lord Voldemort's final Horcrux, making Voldemort fully mortal for the first time in decades. Voldemort immediately experiences overwhelming physical weakness because every safeguard protecting his soul has now been destroyed.

Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort exchange their final spells. Voldemort casts the Killing Curse while Harry Potter casts Expelliarmus. Because the Elder Wand recognizes Harry Potter as its rightful master, Voldemort's spell rebounds. The Elder Wand flies into Harry Potter's hand while Voldemort's own curse destroys him. Lord Voldemort's body disintegrates into ash, marking the permanent end of both the Dark Lord and the Second Wizarding War.

Following the battle, Harry Potter explains the complete history of Elder Wand ownership to Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Harry Potter concludes that the Elder Wand has caused centuries of bloodshed because of humanity's desire for absolute power. Harry Potter deliberately snaps the Elder Wand in half and discards the broken pieces, ending its dangerous legacy forever.

The Battle of Hogwarts permanently transforms the wizarding world. Lord Voldemort's regime collapses. The Death Eaters lose political power. Hogwarts survives despite catastrophic destruction. Survivors mourn countless losses but achieve lasting peace through Voldemort's defeat. Severus Snape's true loyalty is finally understood. Neville Longbottom emerges as one of the greatest heroes of the battle. Narcissa Malfoy's decision to protect her son rather than Voldemort becomes an essential turning point that enables Harry Potter's final victory.

Nineteen years later, lasting peace has replaced the war. Harry Potter and Ginny Weasley marry and become the parents of James Sirius Potter, Albus Severus Potter, and Lily Luna Potter. Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger marry and become the parents of Rose Weasley and Hugo Weasley. Draco Malfoy also marries and raises his son Scorpius Malfoy. The former rivals peacefully reunite at Platform Nine and Three-Quarters while sending their children to Hogwarts.

Harry Potter reassures Albus Severus Potter that being sorted into Slytherin House would not diminish his worth. Harry Potter explains that Albus Severus Potter is named after both Albus Dumbledore and Severus Snape, and Harry Potter declares that Severus Snape was the bravest man he ever knew. This final revelation demonstrates that reconciliation, understanding, and the recognition of hidden sacrifice become the enduring legacy of the war, completing Harry Potter's journey from orphaned child to guardian of a more peaceful wizarding world and establishing the foundation for the events that follow in Harry Potter and the Cursed Child.
