The central event of Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone follows Harry Potter's discovery of the wizarding world, his first year at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, and the first attempt by the weakened Lord Voldemort to regain power by obtaining the Philosopher's Stone. The event establishes the beginning of Harry Potter's journey, introduces the major relationships that shape the future of the wizarding world, and reveals that Lord Voldemort has survived the apparent destruction that occurred when he attempted to murder Harry Potter as an infant.

The event begins during the night of 1 November 1981 on Privet Drive in Little Whinging, Surrey. Albus Dumbledore, the Headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, meets Professor Minerva McGonagall outside number four Privet Drive. The two discuss the recent murders of James Potter and Lily Potter by Lord Voldemort. Rubeus Hagrid arrives carrying the orphaned infant Harry Potter on Sirius Black's flying motorcycle. Minerva McGonagall objects to leaving Harry Potter with the Dursley family because she has secretly observed them in her Animagus form and considers Vernon Dursley, Petunia Dursley, and Dudley Dursley to be unpleasant Muggles. Albus Dumbledore explains that Petunia Dursley is Lily Potter's sister and Harry Potter's only remaining blood relative. Dumbledore further explains that Lily Potter's blood relationship with Petunia creates magical protection that can only function while Harry lives with the Dursley family. Albus Dumbledore leaves Harry Potter together with a letter explaining the circumstances before he, Minerva McGonagall, and Rubeus Hagrid depart.

Approximately ten years later, Harry Potter grows up under the care of Vernon Dursley, Petunia Dursley, and Dudley Dursley. The Dursley family treats Harry Potter as a servant rather than as a family member. Harry Potter experiences neglect, emotional abuse, and constant bullying from Dudley Dursley. Harry Potter remains unaware of his magical heritage because Vernon Dursley and Petunia Dursley deliberately conceal the truth about his parents and the wizarding world.

Shortly before Harry Potter's eleventh birthday, mysterious letters addressed specifically to Harry Potter begin arriving by owl. Vernon Dursley repeatedly prevents Harry from reading the letters and relocates the family several times in an attempt to escape the deliveries. The Dursleys ultimately hide inside a remote hut on an island. At midnight on Harry Potter's eleventh birthday, Rubeus Hagrid enters the hut despite Vernon Dursley's attempts to prevent entry. Rubeus Hagrid gives Harry Potter a birthday cake, presents Harry Potter with his Hogwarts acceptance letter, and reveals that Harry Potter is a wizard. Rubeus Hagrid explains that James Potter and Lily Potter were powerful witches and wizards who were murdered by Lord Voldemort. Hagrid also reveals that Voldemort attempted to murder Harry Potter as a baby but mysteriously failed, leaving Harry Potter with only a lightning-shaped scar while causing Voldemort to lose his physical body and most of his power. When Vernon Dursley insults Albus Dumbledore and continues opposing Harry Potter's magical education, Hagrid magically gives Dudley Dursley a pig's tail as punishment.

The following day, Rubeus Hagrid accompanies Harry Potter to Diagon Alley through the Leaky Cauldron. Harry Potter enters Gringotts Wizarding Bank and discovers that his parents left him a substantial inheritance. Harry Potter purchases school supplies, including his first wand, and receives Hedwig, a snowy owl, from Hagrid as an eleventh birthday gift. Hagrid also retrieves a mysterious package from a high-security vault inside Gringotts on behalf of Albus Dumbledore. Although Harry Potter does not yet know its contents, the retrieval of this package later becomes central to the conflict surrounding the Philosopher's Stone.

Several weeks later, Harry Potter boards the Hogwarts Express at Platform Nine and Three-Quarters located within King's Cross Station. During the train journey, Harry Potter befriends Ron Weasley, a member of the large Weasley wizarding family, and Hermione Granger, a highly intelligent Muggle-born witch. The friendship formed between Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger becomes the foundation of the trio that will repeatedly influence future events throughout the wizarding world.

Upon arriving at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, the first-year students participate in the Sorting Ceremony. The magical Sorting Hat considers placing Harry Potter into Slytherin House because of qualities associated with ambition and power. Harry Potter specifically requests not to join Slytherin because he has already learned that many Dark witches and wizards originated from that house, including Lord Voldemort. The Sorting Hat honors Harry Potter's request and places him into Gryffindor House alongside Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. This decision significantly shapes Harry Potter's friendships, mentors, and future experiences at Hogwarts.

During his first weeks at Hogwarts, Harry Potter begins studying magic while gradually learning more about his parents' history. Harry Potter notices that Professor Severus Snape frequently watches him with apparent hostility, leading Harry to believe that Snape dislikes him for unknown reasons. Harry Potter later discovers that Gringotts Wizarding Bank was broken into shortly after Hagrid removed the mysterious package from its secure vault, suggesting that someone attempted to steal whatever Hagrid had collected.

Harry Potter quickly demonstrates exceptional flying ability while defending Neville Longbottom from Draco Malfoy during a flying lesson. Professor Minerva McGonagall recognizes Harry Potter's natural talent and immediately recruits him as Gryffindor House's Seeker on the Quidditch team. Harry Potter thereby becomes one of the youngest Quidditch players in many years, following in the footsteps of his father, James Potter, who had also played Quidditch.

One evening, Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger accidentally discover a forbidden corridor guarded by a gigantic three-headed dog named Fluffy. Rubeus Hagrid owns Fluffy and placed the creature at Hogwarts specifically to guard an important secret. The discovery convinces the three students that something of great value is being hidden within the school.

During the Halloween feast, Professor Quirinus Quirrell interrupts the celebration by announcing that a mountain troll has entered Hogwarts Castle. Harry Potter and Ron Weasley discover that Hermione Granger remains unaware of the danger because she is hiding in a bathroom after becoming upset earlier that day. Harry Potter and Ron Weasley rescue Hermione by fighting the troll and successfully incapacitating it. Their shared experience transforms Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger from acquaintances into close friends, establishing a partnership based upon mutual trust and loyalty.

Harry Potter participates in his first Quidditch match as Gryffindor's Seeker. During the game, Harry Potter's broomstick suddenly behaves violently and attempts to throw him from the air. Hermione Granger observes Professor Severus Snape staring toward Harry Potter while silently speaking and mistakenly concludes that Snape is cursing the broom. Hermione secretly sets Professor Snape's robes on fire, causing Snape to lose concentration. Harry Potter regains control of the broom, catches the Golden Snitch, and wins the match for Gryffindor. The incident strengthens the trio's suspicion that Severus Snape intends to harm Harry Potter, although this belief later proves incorrect.

While discussing Fluffy, Rubeus Hagrid accidentally mentions the name Nicolas Flamel. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger begin researching Nicolas Flamel without success until Harry Potter later discovers the answer. At Christmas, Harry Potter receives an anonymous gift consisting of his father's Invisibility Cloak. Harry Potter uses the cloak to explore the Restricted Section of the Hogwarts library in search of information regarding Nicolas Flamel.

During one nighttime exploration, Harry Potter discovers the Mirror of Erised. The magical mirror shows Harry Potter a vision of James Potter and Lily Potter standing beside him because Harry's deepest desire is to know and be reunited with his parents. Albus Dumbledore later explains that the Mirror of Erised reveals the deepest desire of a person's heart rather than objective reality. Dumbledore warns Harry Potter that many people become obsessed with the mirror and lose themselves chasing impossible dreams. Dumbledore subsequently relocates the mirror to prevent further misuse.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger eventually learn that Nicolas Flamel is the creator of the Philosopher's Stone. The Philosopher's Stone possesses the ability to produce the Elixir of Life, allowing the drinker to achieve immortality. The trio concludes that Fluffy is guarding the Philosopher's Stone and begins suspecting that Severus Snape intends to steal it for Lord Voldemort.

While visiting Rubeus Hagrid, the trio discovers that Hagrid has acquired a dragon egg. The egg hatches into a Norwegian Ridgeback dragon named Norbert. Because dragons cannot legally be raised at Hogwarts, Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger arrange for Norbert to be transported to Romania. Draco Malfoy observes their activities and informs Professor Minerva McGonagall. Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, Neville Longbottom, and Draco Malfoy all receive detention for breaking school rules.

During detention in the Forbidden Forest, Harry Potter encounters a hooded figure drinking the blood of a dead unicorn. Unicorn blood possesses powerful life-preserving properties, but drinking it curses the drinker. The hooded figure attempts to approach Harry Potter until the centaur Firenze intervenes. Firenze explains that only someone attempting to escape death would commit such an act. Harry Potter concludes that the hooded figure is Lord Voldemort attempting to survive through unnatural means while seeking the Philosopher's Stone to regain a complete body.

Harry Potter later overhears Rubeus Hagrid revealing that Fluffy can be put to sleep by music. Hagrid unknowingly admits that he shared this secret with a stranger in a local pub. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger conclude that Severus Snape deceived Hagrid to obtain Fluffy's weakness. When they discover that Albus Dumbledore has left Hogwarts on urgent business, they believe Severus Snape will immediately attempt to steal the Philosopher's Stone. Acting independently, the three friends decide to protect the stone themselves.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger successfully bypass Fluffy by playing music. They then face a sequence of magical protections designed by Hogwarts professors. Hermione Granger uses magical knowledge to escape Devil's Snare. Harry Potter uses exceptional flying skill to retrieve the correct Flying Key. Ron Weasley sacrifices himself during a life-sized Wizard's Chess game by allowing himself to be struck unconscious so Harry Potter can continue. Hermione Granger remains behind to care for the injured Ron Weasley while Harry Potter proceeds alone into the final chamber.

Harry Potter discovers that Professor Quirinus Quirrell, rather than Professor Severus Snape, has been attempting to steal the Philosopher's Stone throughout the school year. Quirinus Quirrell reveals that he released the mountain troll into Hogwarts and secretly cursed Harry Potter's broom during the Quidditch match. Quirrell further explains that Severus Snape had actually been attempting to protect Harry Potter by performing a counter-curse. This revelation fundamentally changes Harry Potter's understanding of Severus Snape and demonstrates that appearances throughout the school year had been deliberately misleading.

Professor Quirrell forces Harry Potter to stand before the Mirror of Erised. Albus Dumbledore previously enchanted the mirror so that only a person seeking the Philosopher's Stone without intending to use it could obtain it. Harry Potter unexpectedly discovers that the Philosopher's Stone has appeared inside his own pocket. Quirinus Quirrell removes his turban and reveals that the weakened Lord Voldemort survives as a parasitic presence attached to the back of Quirrell's head. Lord Voldemort attempts to persuade Harry Potter to surrender the stone by falsely promising to restore James Potter and Lily Potter to life. Harry Potter refuses.

Quirinus Quirrell attacks Harry Potter in an attempt to seize the Philosopher's Stone. Whenever Quirrell touches Harry Potter, Quirrell's body burns because Lily Potter's sacrificial death created a powerful magical protection based on love that Lord Voldemort cannot overcome. Harry Potter continues resisting until Quirrell's body disintegrates and dies. Lord Voldemort abandons Quirrell's body as a disembodied spirit, passes through Harry Potter, renders Harry unconscious, and escapes to continue searching for another means of returning to power.

Harry Potter awakens in the Hogwarts Hospital Wing where Albus Dumbledore explains the true nature of Lily Potter's sacrifice. Dumbledore explains that Lily Potter willingly gave her life to save Harry Potter and that this act of unconditional love created magical protection remaining within Harry. Albus Dumbledore also explains that Nicolas Flamel and his wife have agreed to destroy the Philosopher's Stone, ensuring that Lord Voldemort can never use it to obtain immortality. Although Nicolas Flamel will eventually die without the Elixir of Life, Dumbledore explains that death is preferable to allowing Voldemort to regain power.

At the end-of-year feast, Slytherin House initially appears to have won the House Cup with the highest number of House Points. Before presenting the trophy, Albus Dumbledore recognizes the courage and achievements demonstrated during the protection of the Philosopher's Stone. Dumbledore awards fifty points to Hermione Granger for exceptional intelligence under dangerous circumstances, fifty points to Ron Weasley for outstanding strategic skill during Wizard's Chess, sixty points to Harry Potter for courage and determination, and ten points to Neville Longbottom for showing the bravery to stand up to his own friends. These additional points give Gryffindor House enough points to surpass Slytherin House and win the House Cup. The victory rewards moral courage, intelligence, friendship, and self-sacrifice rather than personal ambition alone.

The events conclude as the Hogwarts students prepare to return home for the summer. Harry Potter realizes that Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry has become the first place where he has experienced friendship, belonging, acceptance, and love. Harry Potter comes to regard Hogwarts as his true home rather than Privet Drive.

The immediate consequences of these events include the destruction of the Philosopher's Stone, the death of Professor Quirinus Quirrell, the temporary failure of Lord Voldemort's plan to regain a physical body, the strengthening of the friendship between Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger, the revelation that Severus Snape had secretly protected Harry Potter despite appearing hostile, and Gryffindor House winning the House Cup.

The long-term consequences fundamentally reshape the wizarding world. Harry Potter begins understanding his connection to Lord Voldemort, the importance of his mother's sacrificial protection, and the responsibilities associated with his survival. Lord Voldemort remains alive as a weakened spirit, ensuring that future conflicts remain inevitable. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger establish the trust, complementary skills, and loyalty that become essential in later confrontations with Lord Voldemort and his followers. The failure to obtain the Philosopher's Stone forces Lord Voldemort to seek alternative methods of restoring his body and power, directly influencing the events of the subsequent years.
