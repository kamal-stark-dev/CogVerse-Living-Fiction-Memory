Sidney "Sid" Garner is the father of Alan and Tracy, the father-in-law of Doug Billings, and the patriarch of the Garner family. He is portrayed by Jeffrey Tambor. Sid is warm, laid-back, and hip for a man his age — the kind of father-in-law who gives genuine Las Vegas wisdom rather than lecture. He is one of the overarching protagonists of the trilogy, despite appearing mostly in supporting capacity.

Sid lends his prized Mercedes-Benz convertible to Doug for the Las Vegas bachelor party trip in Part I, with specific instructions that only Doug should drive it because "I don't trust Phil, and there's something wrong with Alan." The car is subsequently wrecked in spectacular fashion by Chow's SUV attack. It is later revealed the car was Sid's wedding gift to Doug and Tracy — a fact that reframes the wreckage as both funnier and more painful.

Sid is one of the few characters in the franchise who genuinely sees Alan clearly and loves him anyway. He calls Alan "Sweetie," pays his rent despite Alan's unemployment at age 42, and has been managing his son's mental health and financial needs for decades. By Part III, however, Sid's patience has reached its limit. Alan's purchase of a giraffe — which is decapitated by a highway overpass causing a massive traffic pileup on Interstate 405 — produces a series of checks for damages that significantly depletes Sid's finances. When Alan refuses to get back on his ADHD medication, Sid's anger reaches a peak that causes him to suffer a fatal heart attack, collapsing in front of his wife and housekeeper while Alan, headphones in, misses the entire thing.

Sid's death is the emotional trigger for The Hangover Part III. It forces Alan to confront his own immaturity and eventually leads him to agree to enter rehabilitation, which sets the film's plot in motion. In the film's conclusion, Alan's mental image of his father's ghost — proud of Alan for finally choosing to grow up — is the moment that encourages him to pursue Cassie.

Sid's most famous line from Part I captures the franchise's spirit perfectly: "Remember, what happens in Vegas stays in Vegas. Except for herpes. That shit'll come back with you."
