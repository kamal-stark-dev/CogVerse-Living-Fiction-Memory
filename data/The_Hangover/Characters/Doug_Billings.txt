Douglas Joseph "Doug" Billings is the groom whose bachelor party trip to Las Vegas is the catalyst for the entire Hangover franchise. He is portrayed by Justin Bartha. Doug is the most mild-mannered of the Wolf Pack — sensible, grounded, genuinely kind, and the emotional center of the group's friendships. He is the overarching protagonist of the trilogy, though his actual screen time is ironically the smallest of the four leads.

Doug is married to Tracy Garner-Billings, making Alan his brother-in-law, and works as an archivist in Los Angeles. He is the glue of the Wolf Pack: the person who keeps the group together, manages its excesses, and insists on giving Alan the benefit of the doubt when everyone else has lost patience.

In Part I, Doug disappears for the entire film after being accidentally locked on the roof of Caesars Palace by the group (who couldn't find him because, in their blackout state, they never thought to check the roof). He gets severely sunburned and is found barely in time for his wedding. His first words to Tracy at the altar: "I promise for as long as we're married to never, ever put you through anything like this again."

In Part II, Doug avoids being drugged again (having left the bonfire early with Tracy before Alan's marshmallows took effect) and serves as the group's phone-in support from the resort in Phuket while Phil, Stu, and Alan tear through Bangkok looking for Teddy.

In Part III, Doug is kidnapped by Marshall as collateral to force the Wolf Pack to track down Chow, and is held for most of the film's runtime — another variation on the franchise's running joke that Doug always ends up missing or captive while his friends cause havoc.

Doug is notably one of the only Wolf Pack members who has essentially never met Chow directly, despite Chow being central to every adventure. This running near-miss is part of the franchise's gentle joke that Doug is always in the wrong place at the right time.

His signature quote: "Remember what happens in Vegas stays in Vegas. Except for herpes. That shit'll come back with you" — actually spoken by his father-in-law Sid at the start of Part I, summarizing the trip's philosophy perfectly.
