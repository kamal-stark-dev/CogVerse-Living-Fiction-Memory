Dr. Stuart "Stu" Price is a dentist from Los Angeles, the deuteragonist of the trilogy, and the character who undergoes the most dramatic situational catastrophes across all three films. He is portrayed by Ed Helms. Stu serves as deuteragonist of Part I, main protagonist of Part II (which centers on his wedding), and tritagonist of Part III.

Stu is the responsible, anxious, morally conscientious member of the Wolf Pack — a man who genuinely tries to be sensible but whose alter ego in a blackout state is apparently utterly feral. Sober Stu is uptight, worried about consequences, embarrassed by everything, and deeply invested in being seen as a legitimate doctor (dentists are doctors, he insists, in the face of constant mockery). Blackout Stu has married a stripper, pulled out his own tooth to win a bet, gotten a face tattoo, had sex with a transsexual stripper named Kimmy in Bangkok, and woken up with breast implants after Alan's wedding.

Stu spends Part I in a controlling relationship with Melissa, who cheats on him, bosses him around, and whom Phil openly despises. One of the franchise's most satisfying character moments is Stu standing up to Melissa and dumping her at Doug's wedding at the end of Part I — a moment that Phil greets with visible pride.

Stu's story in Part II centers entirely on his wedding to Lauren, a Thai woman whose surgeon father Fong initially looks down on Stu as insufficiently impressive for his daughter. The entire Bangkok adventure — the tattoo, the missing brother-in-law Teddy, the chaos — culminates in Stu delivering a speech to Fong that reframes the chaos as evidence of his inner "demon," his willingness to throw himself into hell for the people he loves. Fong accepts him.

Stu's relationship with Jade — the Las Vegas stripper he accidentally married in the blackout of Part I — is handled with surprising warmth. He asks her on a date, and by Part III she is married to a doctor, living comfortably, and serves as the group's contact to find Chow's location at Caesars Palace.

Stu is consistently the most emotionally reactive member of the group. He screams, cries, panics, and delivers many of the trilogy's best lines in states of pure incredulous horror: "WHAT THE FUCK IS GOING ON?!", "YOU DRUGGED US AGAIN?!", "I made love to a man with boobies," and "This is a REAL tattoo!"
