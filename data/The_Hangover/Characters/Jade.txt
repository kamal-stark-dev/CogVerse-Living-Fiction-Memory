Jade is a Las Vegas stripper and escort who becomes an accidental participant in the Wolf Pack's first Las Vegas blackout night. She is portrayed by Heather Graham. Jade is warm, non-judgmental, and handles the absurdity of meeting three confused men who cannot remember anything with remarkable composure.

During the blackout of Part I, Jade somehow ended up marrying Stu Price — he gave her his grandmother's wedding ring, known in the group as the "Holocaust ring" because Stu's grandmother had smuggled it through a Nazi concentration camp. Jade also left her infant son Tyler in the group's hotel suite at Caesars Palace during the blackout. When Phil, Stu, and Alan return the baby to her, she kisses Stu on the lips and he asks her on a date despite the fact they are about to get divorced.

Jade is notable as an entirely positive presence in the franchise — a woman from whom the Wolf Pack would reasonably expect judgment but instead receives only kindness and good humor. Her take on her own situation: "I'm a stripper. Well, technically I'm an escort, but stripping's a great way to meet the clients. But that's all in the past, now that I married a doctor."

She returns in Part III, now married to a wealthy doctor, retired from stripping, a present mother to Tyler, and pregnant with another child. She serves as the group's contact to locate Chow at Caesars Palace, helping them identify the penthouse suite he has taken over with drugs and security guards.

Jade represents the franchise's running theme that the strangest encounters of a blackout night can produce genuine human connection. Her brief marriage to Stu is treated not as a joke but as a small, real thing — he liked her, she liked him, the circumstances were impossible, and they both moved on to better versions of their lives.
