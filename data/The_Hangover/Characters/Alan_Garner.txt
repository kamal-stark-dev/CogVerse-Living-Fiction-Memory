Alan Garner is the son of Sid and Linda Garner, the younger brother of Tracy Garner-Billings, and Doug's brother-in-law. He is portrayed by Zach Galifianakis. Alan is the tritagonist of the trilogy in Parts I and II, and becomes the main protagonist of Part III. He is arguably the most beloved and iconic character in the franchise and its signature comedic presence.

Alan is childlike, strange, deeply loyal, and operates by a completely idiosyncratic moral code that makes perfect sense to him and baffles everyone else. He lives with his parents at age 42, is unemployed, takes ADHD medication that he intermittently stops taking, is technically not allowed within 200 feet of a school or a Chuck E. Cheese, and considers the Wolf Pack to be his entire world. He refers to the group as "my wolf pack" in a deeply sincere monologue in Part I that becomes one of the most quoted speeches in the franchise: "I'm not supposed to have friends, but this is my wolf pack."

Despite his apparent stupidity, Alan is consistently the character who saves the group. He taught himself blackjack from a book and wins $80,000 at the casino in Part I. He has a spiritual epiphany during meditation at a Bangkok monastery in Part II that helps locate Teddy. He is the only one still in contact with Chow by Part III, which makes him the critical link in every mission.

Alan's most catastrophic act across the trilogy is drugging his friends twice. In Part I, he accidentally put drugs in their drinks during the rooftop toast (having crushed a pill he obtained from a man named Black Doug, which turned out to be Roofies, not Ecstasy). In Part II, he deliberately put ADHD medication and muscle relaxants into marshmallows intending only to knock out Teddy, but the whole group ate them. Both acts are presented as genuine attempts to help (in his own strange logic) rather than malice.

Alan's relationship with Leslie Chow is the franchise's most unexpected friendship. The two maintain contact between films, write letters to each other while Chow is in prison, and genuinely love each other in the chaotic way that only very strange people can. In Part III, Alan's decision to end his friendship with Chow — recognizing Chow's bad influence — represents his greatest act of personal growth.

Alan's character arc across the trilogy is one of genuine maturation. He begins as an isolated, medicated man-child living at his parents' expense. He ends the trilogy married to Cassie, having recognized his own limitations, having come to terms with his father Sid's death, and having found the confidence to lead his own life while keeping the Wolf Pack's weekly plans intact.

His iconic lines include: "I tend to think of myself as a one-man wolf pack," "Not at the table, Carlos," "I fudged up," and a recurring deadpan response to increasingly horrifying situations: "Classic!"
