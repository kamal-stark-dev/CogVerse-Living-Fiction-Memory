Philip "Phil" Wenneck is the de facto leader of the Wolf Pack, the best friend of Doug Billings since childhood, and the most charismatic and reckless member of the group. Phil is portrayed by Bradley Cooper. He is the main protagonist of the trilogy, serving as the central point of view character across all three films.

Phil works as a teacher at a private school in Los Angeles, a job he openly resents. He is married to Stephanie and has two children, Eli and Vicky. Despite his suburban family life, Phil constantly seeks escape — he loves bachelor parties, is notoriously careless, and occasionally steals money from his students under the pretense of a school field trip to fund the Vegas trip. His philosophy is that life is too short to be responsible, which puts him directly at odds with Stu and, occasionally, Doug.

Phil is the most physically confident of the Wolf Pack. He drives drunk, stands on the hoods of moving cars, handles violent criminals with relative calm, and never seems genuinely panicked by the chaos around him. He is quick-witted, loud, and instinctively takes charge when the group needs direction. He is fiercely protective of his friends even when mocking them — his ribbing of Stu about the dentist-vs-doctor distinction is relentless, but he is genuinely proud of Stu when he finally stands up to his controlling ex-girlfriend Melissa.

Phil's relationship with Alan is one of the franchise's emotional cores. Alan idolizes Phil to an almost embarrassing degree, mimicking his appearance and behavior, and considers him his best friend. Phil is the most tolerant of Alan's erratic, often dangerous behavior, and shows genuine affection for him. In The Hangover Part III, when Alan tearfully tells Phil he can no longer be his hero, Phil simply accepts it without embarrassment — a moment that captures the warmth beneath Phil's otherwise selfish exterior.

Across the trilogy, Phil accumulates an impressive catalogue of injuries and chaos: he is tased in the groin, clawed by a tiger, hit with a crowbar, shot in the left shoulder in Bangkok, and punched and headbutted by Chow in Vegas. He survives all of it with characteristic nonchalance.

Phil's most memorable lines reflect his worldview: "We fucked up" (said after every catastrophe), "We are back. We are FUCKING BACK!!", and his sardonic running commentary on everything the Wolf Pack encounters. Phil is the man you call when the situation is already a disaster and someone needs to take charge of the wreckage.
