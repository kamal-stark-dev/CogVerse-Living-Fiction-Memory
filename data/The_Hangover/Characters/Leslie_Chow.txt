Leslie Chow, universally known as Mr. Chow, is an international criminal, drug lord, smuggler, gun dealer, and career chaos-agent who serves as the main antagonist of Part I, a supporting character in Part II, and the deuteragonist villain of Part III. He is portrayed by Ken Jeong and is the franchise's breakout secondary character. Chow was born in Taiwan and based in various locations across the world depending on which criminal enterprise he is currently running.

Chow first appeared jumping naked from the trunk of Sid Garner's Mercedes and attacking Phil, Stu, and Alan with a crowbar, having been accidentally locked in the car during the blackout. He escalated to ramming their car, kidnapping (the wrong) Doug, and threatening their lives over $80,000 in casino chips. By the end of Part I he is mostly gone, but the pattern was established: wherever Chow appears, catastrophe follows at maximum volume.

Chow's personality combines genuine psychopathic tendencies with anarchic warmth. He is upbeat, outgoing, and surprisingly loyal to people he considers friends. He keeps the party going at any cost, including illegal ones. He is one of the most cunning characters in the franchise — always several steps ahead — while simultaneously being one of the most reckless. He frequently snorts enormous amounts of cocaine on screen, once causing his heart to stop completely (he survived, woke up in an ice freezer, and immediately attacked the group again).

Chow's friendship with Alan is the franchise's most unlikely emotional bond. The two correspond by letter while Chow is imprisoned in Thailand. Alan considers Chow one of his best friends, second only to Phil. In Part III, when Alan ends their friendship for the good of his own mental health, Chow is genuinely hurt and confused — one of the few moments the franchise lets Chow's real feelings show beneath the chaos.

Chow's catchphrase — "Toodaloo, motherfuckers!" — is used at the end of each film as his exit line, and his declaration "We had a sick night, bitches!" is how he opens the post-credits scene of Part III, naked and wielding a katana after apparently drugging Alan's wedding cake.

Chow is the trilogy's wild-card engine: the source of most of the Wolf Pack's worst nights, and paradoxically the reason several of those nights resolved. Without Chow's gold (Part III), Chow's speedboat (Part II), and Chow's stolen chips (Part I), the Wolf Pack would never have gotten home. He is simultaneously the problem and the solution.
