The second Hangover event takes place in Thailand, two years after the Las Vegas blackout. Stu is marrying Lauren, a Thai woman whose academically accomplished younger brother Teddy is set to attend med school at Stanford. To avoid another Las Vegas-style disaster, Stu insists on a "bachelor brunch" at an IHOP instead of a bachelor party — chocolate chip pancakes, no alcohol, no debauchery. This plan lasts approximately one evening.

The Wolf Pack, along with Teddy and Lauren, have a "quiet" bonfire on the beach at the resort in Phuket. Alan, intending to incapacitate Teddy alone so the group could enjoy the weekend without him, laces rice crispy marshmallows with his ADHD medication and muscle relaxants. Everyone eats them. The group blacks out.

Phil, Stu, and Alan wake up in a filthy hotel room in Bangkok — hundreds of miles from the resort. The room contains: a drug-dealing Capuchin monkey wearing a Rolling Stones vest; Leslie Chow, whom Alan has apparently stayed in contact with and who stowed away on the plane to Thailand; Alan's head is completely shaved; Stu has a copy of Mike Tyson's iconic facial tattoo permanently inked on the left side of his face. Teddy is missing. All that remains of him is a severed finger in a bowl of ice.

The Bangkok adventure involves: Chow snorting so much cocaine that his heart stops (they lock his body in a hotel ice machine, from which he later escapes); discovering the drug-dealing monkey; being shot at by Russian mobsters demanding the monkey back (Phil is shot in the shoulder and nearly dies); Alan confessing to the drugging; discovering Stu had sex with a transsexual stripper named Kimmy ("I made love to a man with boobies"); confronting Kingsley, who turns out to be an Interpol undercover agent; getting Chow arrested; and ultimately discovering that Teddy had been trapped in the hotel elevator the entire time after a power cut, having lost his finger in a game of five-finger fillet.

The group uses Chow's speedboat, the "Perfect Life," to race back to the resort in time for the wedding. Stu delivers an impassioned speech to Lauren's skeptical father demonstrating that he is not the boring dentist he appears to be, but a man with a "demon" — wild, loyal, capable of surviving Bangkok — who is exactly the right person for Lauren. Lauren's father accepts him. Stu and Lauren get married. Mike Tyson performs a musical number as Alan's wedding gift.

The film ends with the group discovering the photos from the night, Teddy having recharged his phone. The images reveal what happened — including, somehow, the monkey appearing in almost every photo — and the group agrees to delete the evidence.

The Bangkok blackout is structurally identical to Las Vegas but darker in tone and setting. Bangkok represents the film's commentary on how consequences compound: the Wolf Pack knows exactly how badly this can go, and it goes worse.
