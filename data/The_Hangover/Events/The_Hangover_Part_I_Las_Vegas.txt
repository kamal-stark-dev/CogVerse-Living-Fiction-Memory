The original Hangover event is the inciting incident of the entire franchise: the Wolf Pack's bachelor party trip to Las Vegas for Doug Billings's wedding, which descends into the single most catastrophic and hilarious night in the group's history.

The trip begins on the roof of Caesars Palace, where Phil, Stu, Alan, and Doug make a toast to a night they will never forget. The next morning, Phil, Stu, and Alan wake up in their completely destroyed luxury hotel suite with no memory of anything. The suite contains: a live Bengal tiger in the bathroom; an infant baby named Tyler in the closet; a chicken wandering the room; Stu is missing a front tooth; the room itself has been systematically devastated. Doug is missing entirely.

Over the course of the film, the trio piece together what happened the previous night through a combination of physical clues, encounters with people they apparently met, and a photo card Alan eventually finds in the Mercedes. What they discover:

They stole a police car and went joyriding in it. They stole Mike Tyson's pet Bengal tiger from his mansion and transported it to their hotel bathroom. Alan purchased drugs from a man named Black Doug, believing them to be Ecstasy but actually obtaining Roofies (flunitrazepam) — a powerful sedative — which he crushed into everyone's drinks on the roof. Stu married Jade, a stripper and escort, in a Las Vegas wedding chapel, giving her his grandmother's Holocaust ring. Stu also pulled out his own front tooth to win a bet against Alan. Doug was accidentally locked on the roof of Caesars Palace at some point and spent the entire night sunburning in the desert heat, completely unable to attract attention from below.

The Wolf Pack's attempts to reconstruct the night include: returning the tiger to Mike Tyson (who punches Alan in the face), being tased by Las Vegas police, playing blackjack using Alan's self-taught card counting technique to win $80,000, encountering Leslie Chow jumping naked from their car trunk and attacking them with a crowbar, and negotiating with Chow for Doug's life — only to discover Chow had kidnapped the wrong Doug (a drug dealer named Black Doug, not their friend).

The real Doug is found on the roof. The group drives back to Pasadena at high speed, arriving barely in time for the wedding. Stu breaks up with Melissa at the reception, delivers the best man speech, and asks Jade on a date. The group discovers Alan's camera with the photos from the night and agree to look at them once, then delete them forever.

The first film established the franchise's core formula: the blackout mystery, the absurd morning-after inventory of chaos, the race against a wedding deadline, and the emotional catharsis of a group of friends who would do anything for each other. Its tone — equal parts comedy and genuine warmth — made The Hangover the highest-grossing R-rated comedy ever made at the time of release.
