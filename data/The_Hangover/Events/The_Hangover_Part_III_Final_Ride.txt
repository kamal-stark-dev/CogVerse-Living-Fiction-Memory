The third and final Hangover event departs from the franchise formula deliberately: there is no bachelor party, no wedding (until the end), and no blackout — at least not as the film's central structural device. Instead, Part III is a crime thriller road-movie, following the Wolf Pack across California, Mexico, and Las Vegas as they are coerced by a dangerous criminal to retrieve stolen gold from Leslie Chow.

The trigger is Alan. Having been off his ADHD medication for six months, Alan purchases a giraffe online and tows it through Irvine, California on a highway, decapitating it on an overpass and causing a multi-car pileup. The resulting damages bills destroy Sid Garner's savings. When Sid confronts Alan about refusing to take responsibility, Alan puts on his headphones to drown out the lecture — and Sid collapses from a fatal heart attack, dying in front of his wife and housekeeper while Alan hears nothing.

After Sid's funeral, Doug organizes a Wolf Pack intervention and convinces Alan to enter a rehabilitation facility in Arizona. The group drives there together. On the highway, they are rammed off the road and kidnapped by Marshall, a dangerous crime boss played by John Goodman, and his henchman Black Doug. Marshall reveals that Leslie Chow stole $21 million in gold from his $42 million vault, and uses Doug as collateral to force Phil, Stu, and Alan to track Chow down.

The chase takes the group to Tijuana (where Chow has been communicating with Alan), back through Mexico (where they attempt to drug Chow, fail, and are locked in a basement by him after he double-crosses them), and ultimately to Las Vegas, where Chow has taken over the penthouse suite of Caesars Palace. Phil and Alan parachute down to Chow's balcony from the roof; Chow parachutes off and lands on Stu's limo. Stu puts Chow in the trunk.

In the desert at dawn, they hand Chow over to Marshall. Alan, secretly, had helped Chow escape the trunk — so when Marshall shoots the limo's trunk to kill Chow, Chow emerges alive and kills Marshall and his men. Chow offers Alan a gold brick; Alan declines and formally ends their friendship, recognizing Chow as a genuinely unhealthy influence. Chow, genuinely hurt, watches the Wolf Pack leave.

Alan stays behind in Las Vegas to ask Cassie, the pawn shop owner, on a date. Six months later, Alan and Cassie marry. Alan announces he is leaving the Wolf Pack — though he has arranged weekly plans for the group to continue. The four walk to the wedding in a montage recapping the trilogy.

Post-credits: Everyone wakes up in a trashed hotel room at Alan and Cassie's wedding. Stu has breast implants. Alan remembers the wedding cake was Chow's gift. Chow bursts out of the next room naked, holding a katana, declaring "We had a sick night, bitches!" The drug-dealing monkey drops onto Stu's shoulder.

The final film is the franchise's most thematically complete chapter: Alan grows up, Chow gets defeated but survives, and the Wolf Pack's next chapter begins — older, slower, and still somehow ending up in a destroyed hotel room with no memory of the night before.
