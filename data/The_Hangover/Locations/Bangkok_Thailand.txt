Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, is the primary setting of The Hangover Part II and the franchise's second great blackout city. It is described by the film as one of the most chaotic, unpredictable, and overwhelming cities on Earth — a place where anything can happen and most of it does.

The Wolf Pack wakes up in a filthy, anonymous hotel room in Bangkok after a blackout that should not have happened: Stu had specifically planned a conservative bachelor brunch rather than a party to avoid exactly this outcome. The city's sheer scale and labyrinthine energy make it perfectly suited to the franchise's mystery format: Bangkok is a city where you can genuinely disappear, where every street contains a new crisis, and where the rules of ordinary life are suspended.

Bangkok's role in the film extends across several key environments: the anonymous hotel room where the group awakens; the streets of the city where the group pursues the drug-dealing monkey; the Lebua Hotel (the famous rooftop bar) where the Interpol sting operation takes place; a Buddhist monastery where a monk hits Alan with a stick for speaking; a veterinary clinic where the shot monkey is treated; and the hotel elevator where Teddy had been trapped the entire film.

The city is presented as beautiful, overwhelming, and completely indifferent to the Wolf Pack's crisis. It is simultaneously a real place with genuine cultural texture (Buddhist monks, river transport, the cityscape) and a heightened comedic nightmare that amplifies every chaos the group brings into it.

Bangkok's most iconic contribution to the franchise: Stu wakes up with a permanent tattoo — a replica of Mike Tyson's tribal face tattoo — on the left side of his face, applied during the blackout. The tattoo becomes the film's central visual symbol of the cost of a lost night in an unforgiving city.
