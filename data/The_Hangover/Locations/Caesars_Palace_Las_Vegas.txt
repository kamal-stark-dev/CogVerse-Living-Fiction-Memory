Caesars Palace is the iconic Las Vegas hotel and casino that serves as the primary location of The Hangover Part I and a key location in Part III. It is the physical stage for the franchise's most legendary disaster — and, fittingly, the place the Wolf Pack keeps returning to.

In Part I, the Wolf Pack books a luxury villa suite at Caesars Palace for Doug's bachelor party. The suite costs thousands per night and is equipped with everything required for a spectacular celebration. By morning, it has been systematically destroyed: furniture overturned, the bathroom containing a Bengal tiger, a chicken wandering the floors, a baby in the closet. The total amount of damage inflicted on the suite during the blackout night is never tallied but is clearly enormous.

The roof of Caesars Palace is where the evening begins — the Wolf Pack's iconic rooftop toast — and where it ends, in a sense: Doug is found on the roof after spending the entire night trapped there, severely sunburned, unable to attract attention from the street below.

The casino floor of Caesars is where Alan applies his self-taught blackjack card-counting to win $80,000, generating the funds needed to pay off Chow and (they believe) secure Doug's release. The casino's scale and anonymity are part of what makes Las Vegas the perfect city for the franchise: there is nowhere in the world where a wolf pack of four men can create this much chaos and have it feel, somehow, like a normal Friday night.

In Part III, Chow takes over the Caesars Palace penthouse suite, surrounding himself with drugs, security guards, and an atmosphere of fortified debauchery. Phil and Alan gain access by rappelling down from the roof to the penthouse balcony — a callback to the original film's rooftop setting, closing a geographic loop across the trilogy.

Caesars Palace represents the franchise's central emotional location: where the chaos began, where it keeps returning, and where the legend of the Wolf Pack's worst nights is written in ruined furniture and tiger scratch marks.
