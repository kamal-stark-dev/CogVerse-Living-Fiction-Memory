The Wolf Pack is the informal name for the core friend group of Phil Wenneck, Alan Garner, Stu Price, and Doug Billings — the four central characters of The Hangover trilogy. The name is coined by Alan in the first film during one of the most sincerely delivered monologues in the franchise, in which he describes himself as a "one-man wolf pack" who has now accepted Phil, Stu, and Doug into it.

The Wolf Pack is not a location in the physical sense but functions as the franchise's primary relational universe — the social structure that every event, crisis, and character arc orbits. The term captures something true about the group dynamic: they run together, they protect each other, they wreak collective havoc, and their loyalty to one another is the one constant across all three films and all three catastrophic adventures.

The Wolf Pack's internal hierarchy is informal but consistent: Phil leads by charisma and confidence. Stu follows reluctantly but shows up every time. Doug anchors the group emotionally and is the most consistently sensible. Alan loves the group more intensely than any of them love anything, which makes him simultaneously its most dangerous and most devoted member.

The Wolf Pack has survived: two massive blackouts across two of the world's most overwhelming cities; a tiger, a stolen police car, a drug-dealing monkey, and a kidnapping by an armed criminal organization; face tattoos, lost teeth, and breast implants; and the death of their patriarch Sid Garner.

At the end of Part III, Alan formally leaves the Wolf Pack to begin his married life — but arranges weekly group gatherings. The franchise closes with the implication that the Wolf Pack continues not as the chaos engine it was, but as something more lasting: a group of men who, against all reasonable probability, chose each other and kept choosing each other through everything.

The Wolf Pack's identity is the Hangover universe's answer to the question every Cognee query about this franchise ultimately raises: "How did they survive all of this?" They survived because none of them would leave without the others.
