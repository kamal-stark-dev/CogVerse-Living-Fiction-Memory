The series finale of Breaking Bad, titled "Felina" (an anagram of "finale" and a reference to the Marty Robbins song "El Paso"), depicted Walter White's return from New Hampshire exile and his last day alive. It was Walt's birthday — September 7, 2010 — exactly two years after his cancer diagnosis. He died the same day he turned 52.

Walt left his New Hampshire cabin after watching Elliott and Gretchen Schwartz on a Charlie Rose interview dismiss his contributions to Gray Matter Technologies. His ego, as always, was the mechanism that moved him. He drove back to Albuquerque in a stolen car.

He appeared first at the Schwartz estate, where he held them at gunpoint and tasked them with ensuring his drug money — $9 million — would reach Walter White Jr. on his 18th birthday through their charitable foundation. To guarantee their compliance, he told them he had hired hitmen to watch them indefinitely. The "hitmen" were Badger and Skinny Pete with laser pointers. Walt paid them $200,000 for the performance. The con worked.

Walt visited Skyler at her small apartment near Eubank, gave her the GPS coordinates of Hank and Gomez's burial site as leverage with prosecutors, and — finally — told her the truth she had been waiting for: "I did it for me. I liked it. I was good at it. And I was really... alive." Skyler allowed him to see Holly one last time. He left before Walt Jr. arrived home, watching his son through a window from across the street.

At a diner, Walt poisoned Lydia Rodarte-Quayle's Stevia with ricin — the one packet he knew she would pick, the one habit he had observed for long enough to exploit. He confirmed the dose afterward by phone: Lydia died thinking she had the flu.

At Jack's compound, Walt demonstrated a remotely activated M60 machine gun mounted in the trunk of a car. When the trunk opened by remote, the gun swept the room, killing Jack's entire gang in seconds. Walt shielded Jesse from the gunfire by tackling him to the floor, taking a bullet himself in the process. Jesse strangled Todd with his handcuffs. Walt shot Jack in the head mid-sentence — the same way Jack had executed Hank.

Walt gave Jesse his gun and told him to shoot him. Jesse refused unless Walt asked for it. Walt admitted he wanted to die. Jesse looked at Walt's wound — already fatal — and told him to do it himself. He drove away through the compound gate, laughing and crying simultaneously. Free.

Walt walked into the superlab — now operated by Jack's gang — and looked at the equipment with a soft, blissful expression. He placed his bloodied hand on a steel tank. His last moments were spent touching chemistry equipment, in the place where he had felt most alive. The police found him there, dead on the floor, with a faint smile on his face.

Walter White died on his 52nd birthday, alone, in a lab, having provided for his family's financial future, destroyed his enemies, freed Jesse, and told Skyler the truth. Whether this constitutes redemption or simply a man who arranged things to his satisfaction at the end is the question the series leaves open, and refuses to answer.
