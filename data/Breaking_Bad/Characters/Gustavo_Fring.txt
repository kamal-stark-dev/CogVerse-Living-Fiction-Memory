Gustavo Fring, born circa 1958 in Chile, was a Chilean-American restaurant entrepreneur, civic philanthropist, and the most sophisticated drug kingpin in American history before Walter White surpassed him. Gus Fring operated his methamphetamine distribution network beneath an almost impenetrable public identity: proprietor of the Los Pollos Hermanos restaurant chain, supporter of the DEA's Albuquerque office, hospital board member, and model businessman. He hid in plain sight so effectively that DEA agents who investigated him considered him beyond suspicion. Gus himself described his strategy to Walter White as: "I hide in plain sight, same as you."

Gus Fring's entire criminal career was an act of revenge. In 1989 in Mexico, when he and his closest partner and romantic companion Maximino "Max" Arciniega approached cartel boss Don Eladio Vuente with a proposal to distribute their high-quality meth, Eladio had Hector Salamanca shoot Max in the head as punishment for dealing under the cartel's nose without permission. Gus was forced to watch Max die by the side of a swimming pool while Eladio taunted him. Eladio spared Gus's life because he "knew who Gus really was" — a reference to Gus's mysterious, never-fully-revealed past in Chile that gave him political or military protection. Everything that followed — the restaurant chain, the superlab, the distribution empire, the patient alliance-building — was in service of the singular goal of destroying Eladio, the Salamanca family, and the entire cartel structure that had murdered Max.

Gus Fring's operational genius was his patience and his meticulous control of perception. He cultivated Gale Boetticher as a chemist, commissioned the construction of a state-of-the-art industrial superlab beneath the Lavandería Brillante laundry, built a distribution network through Los Pollos Hermanos trucks, and laundered money through the German conglomerate Madrigal Electromotive GmbH. He employed Mike Ehrmantraut as his chief enforcer and kept Hector Salamanca alive but humiliated for decades, visiting the nursing home regularly to taunt him with the deaths of his family, saving Hector as the last victim because he wanted Hector to know he had buried every Salamanca before he died.

Gus's relationship with Walter White was a power struggle between two men of equal intelligence and far less equal temperament. Gus admired Walt's chemistry and recognized the genius of Blue Sky, but never trusted Walt's judgment, ego, or capacity for self-control. His plan was always to replace Walt with Gale once Gale had mastered the formula. Walt recognized this, ordered Gale's murder, and made himself irreplaceable. From that point, Gus and Walt were in a cold war that ended with Walt engineering Gus's death.

Gustavo Fring was killed on July 15, 2009, in the explosion of a pipe bomb placed beneath Hector Salamanca's wheelchair at the Casa Tranquila nursing home. Walt had orchestrated the assassination by exploiting the ancient mutual hatred between Gus and Hector — convincing Hector to serve as the bomb's trigger. Gus walked out of Hector's room after the explosion, straightened his tie, and collapsed. The camera revealed half of his face had been blown off. He was dead before he hit the floor.

Gus Fring's personality combined genuine warmth in his personal relationships with ruthless coldness in professional ones. He killed Victor by slitting his throat with a box cutter to demonstrate control over Walt and Jesse. He poisoned an entire cartel dinner party. He had his own employee Werner Ziegler shot to protect the secret of the superlab. He told Gale that "a man provides for his family" — the same language Walt used to justify himself — and he meant it in a completely different context: Gus was providing, always, for the memory of Max. Every empire he built was a monument to the man Hector Salamanca killed by a poolside.
