The events of Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1 begin after the death of Albus Dumbledore and the collapse of the previous balance of power in the Wizarding World. Lord Voldemort has openly returned to power, and the Second Wizarding War has entered its most dangerous stage. The Ministry of Magic is no longer capable of protecting the public, and Voldemort's followers actively pursue control over both wizarding society and the Muggle world. Harry Potter accepts that his final mission is no longer to complete his education at Hogwarts but to destroy Lord Voldemort by locating and eliminating every Horcrux that anchors Voldemort's fragmented soul to the world of the living.

Before Harry Potter leaves his childhood home, Rufus Scrimgeour serves as Minister for Magic and publicly acknowledges Lord Voldemort's return, reversing the previous Ministry administration's denial of the threat. Despite these assurances, the Ministry is already vulnerable to infiltration by Death Eaters. Harry Potter remains at 4 Privet Drive under magical protection until his seventeenth birthday, after which those protections expire. Hermione Granger prepares for the coming war by casting the Obliviate Memory Charm on her parents. Hermione Granger erases every memory her parents possess of her existence and removes all physical evidence of herself from their family photographs. Hermione Granger sacrifices her relationship with her parents in order to protect them from Voldemort and the Death Eaters. Ron Weasley prepares to accompany Harry Potter despite fully understanding the dangers of the mission.

At Malfoy Manor, Lord Voldemort gathers his followers for a strategic meeting. Participants include Severus Snape, Lucius Malfoy, Narcissa Malfoy, Bellatrix Lestrange, Peter Pettigrew, and numerous Death Eaters. Severus Snape secretly provides Lord Voldemort with the planned date and time of Harry Potter's removal from Privet Drive. Lord Voldemort announces that Harry Potter must be delivered alive because Voldemort personally intends to kill Harry. Lord Voldemort explains that Harry Potter's wand and Voldemort's own wand share identical phoenix feather cores, preventing a decisive duel between them. Lord Voldemort therefore confiscates Lucius Malfoy's wand for his own use, humiliating Lucius Malfoy for his previous failures. During the meeting, Charity Burbage, the Muggle Studies professor at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, is suspended above the table as a prisoner. Charity Burbage defends peaceful coexistence between wizards and Muggles and appeals to Severus Snape for help because of their previous friendship. Severus Snape does not intervene. Lord Voldemort murders Charity Burbage with the Killing Curse, and Nagini devours her body. This execution demonstrates Voldemort's absolute control over the Death Eaters and his ideology of wizarding supremacy.

Members of the Order of the Phoenix arrive at Privet Drive to escort Harry Potter to safety. Participants include Alastor Moody, Rubeus Hagrid, Arthur Weasley, Remus Lupin, Nymphadora Tonks, Kingsley Shacklebolt, Bill Weasley, Fleur Delacour, Fred Weasley, George Weasley, Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, Mundungus Fletcher, and others. To confuse Voldemort's forces, six volunteers drink Polyjuice Potion and transform into physical copies of Harry Potter. This strategy becomes known as the Battle of the Seven Potters. Harry Potter travels with Rubeus Hagrid aboard Sirius Black's enchanted motorcycle. Death Eaters ambush the escort force after learning of the operation. During the battle, Hedwig is killed while protecting Harry Potter. Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody is killed after Mundungus Fletcher abandons the mission by Apparating away in fear. George Weasley loses one ear after Severus Snape accidentally cuts it off while attempting to protect Lupin from another Death Eater. Lord Voldemort personally attacks Harry Potter, but Harry's wand unexpectedly acts independently and destroys Lucius Malfoy's borrowed wand. The Order of the Phoenix succeeds in bringing Harry Potter safely to the Burrow despite suffering severe losses.

Following the battle, Harry Potter experiences guilt because members of the Order of the Phoenix were injured or killed while protecting him. Ron Weasley convinces Harry Potter that the war is larger than any one individual and that Harry must continue fighting alongside his friends instead of facing Voldemort alone. This conversation reinforces the central theme that Harry Potter's greatest strength comes from friendship and loyalty rather than individual power.

Rufus Scrimgeour visits the Burrow to execute Albus Dumbledore's will. Dumbledore leaves Ron Weasley the Deluminator, an enchanted device capable of extinguishing, storing, and later releasing sources of light. Dumbledore leaves Hermione Granger his personal copy of The Tales of Beedle the Bard, a collection of wizarding fairy tales that later becomes essential to understanding the Deathly Hallows. Dumbledore leaves Harry Potter the Golden Snitch that Harry caught during his first Quidditch match at Hogwarts. Harry Potter later learns that the Snitch possesses flesh memory and opens only for the individual who first caught it. Dumbledore also intends Harry Potter to inherit the Sword of Godric Gryffindor, but Rufus Scrimgeour refuses because the sword belongs to Hogwarts and has disappeared.

Bill Weasley marries Fleur Delacour at the Burrow. The wedding briefly reunites members of the Order of the Phoenix and Harry Potter's allies. Harry Potter meets Xenophilius Lovegood, editor of The Quibbler, and notices a mysterious triangular symbol hanging around Xenophilius Lovegood's neck. Harry Potter also speaks with Elphias Doge and Aunt Muriel. Aunt Muriel reveals information about Albus Dumbledore's family that Harry never previously knew, including the existence of Aberforth Dumbledore and Dumbledore's complicated personal history. Kingsley Shacklebolt interrupts the celebration by sending a Patronus message announcing that Rufus Scrimgeour has been murdered, the Ministry of Magic has fallen, and Death Eaters are approaching. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger immediately Apparate away to escape capture.

After escaping into Muggle London, Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger are attacked inside a café by Antonin Dolohov and Thorfinn Rowle, who have disguised themselves as Muggle workers. The trio defeats both Death Eaters and erases their memories before seeking refuge at 12 Grimmauld Place, the ancestral home of the Black family and headquarters of the Order of the Phoenix.

At Grimmauld Place, Harry Potter discovers that the mysterious individual identified only as "R.A.B." is Regulus Arcturus Black, the younger brother of Sirius Black. Kreacher, the Black family house-elf, explains that Regulus Black secretly opposed Lord Voldemort after discovering the true nature of the Horcruxes. Regulus Black ordered Kreacher to help retrieve Salazar Slytherin's locket from Voldemort's hidden cave. Regulus Black died attempting to destroy the Horcrux after replacing it with a fake locket containing a message addressed to Voldemort. Kreacher later reveals that Mundungus Fletcher stole the authentic locket from Grimmauld Place and that Mundungus Fletcher traded it to Dolores Umbridge. Harry Potter orders Kreacher to locate Mundungus Fletcher, strengthening Harry's relationship with the house-elf through respect rather than abuse.

Meanwhile, Voldemort's followers seize complete control of the Ministry of Magic. Rufus Scrimgeour is replaced by Pius Thicknesse, who serves as Minister for Magic under Death Eater control. Dolores Umbridge participates in persecuting Muggle-born witches and wizards through sham legal proceedings that falsely accuse them of stealing magical ability. Harry Potter is declared "Undesirable Number One," making him the primary target of the Ministry's nationwide manhunt.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger infiltrate the Ministry using Polyjuice Potion. Harry Potter impersonates Albert Runcorn, Ron Weasley impersonates Reginald Cattermole, and Hermione Granger impersonates Mafalda Hopkirk. Harry Potter discovers evidence that the Ministry monitors his friends and their families. Hermione Granger witnesses Dolores Umbridge presiding over cruel trials that imprison innocent Muggle-born witches and wizards. Harry Potter stuns Dolores Umbridge and recovers Salazar Slytherin's locket. The trio narrowly escapes after their disguises fail.

The trio establishes a hidden campsite in the Forest of Dean. Hermione Granger casts multiple protective enchantments around their tent to prevent detection. Ron Weasley suffers from Splinching after their escape and must recover before continuing the mission. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger discover that the recovered locket is genuinely one of Voldemort's Horcruxes, but they cannot destroy it because ordinary magic is ineffective against Horcruxes.

The Horcrux begins influencing the emotions of its bearer. The fragment of Voldemort's soul inside the locket amplifies fear, jealousy, resentment, anger, and hopelessness. Ron Weasley suffers the greatest psychological effects because of his insecurities regarding Harry Potter's fame, Hermione Granger's affection, and concern for the safety of the Weasley family. As weeks pass without success in locating additional Horcruxes, Ron Weasley argues with Harry Potter and Hermione Granger before abandoning the mission. Hermione Granger chooses to remain with Harry Potter despite her heartbreak over Ron Weasley's departure.

While searching for clues, Hermione Granger realizes that Goblin-made objects permanently absorb substances that increase their effectiveness. Because the Sword of Godric Gryffindor previously killed the Basilisk of Salazar Slytherin, the sword absorbed basilisk venom. Basilisk venom is one of the few substances capable of destroying Horcruxes. This realization establishes the Sword of Gryffindor as a critical weapon in the war against Voldemort.

Harry Potter experiments with the Golden Snitch and discovers the inscription "I open at the close" after touching it with his lips. Neither Harry Potter nor Hermione Granger immediately understands the meaning of the message. Hermione Granger also studies The Tales of Beedle the Bard and repeatedly encounters the mysterious triangular symbol that appeared at Bill Weasley and Fleur Delacour's wedding.

Harry Potter and Hermione Granger travel to Godric's Hollow on Christmas Eve because Bathilda Bagshot once knew Albus Dumbledore and may possess information about the Sword of Gryffindor. Harry Potter visits the graves of James Potter and Lily Potter, confronting the reality of his parents' deaths and receiving emotional comfort from Hermione Granger. Bathilda Bagshot secretly serves as a disguise for Nagini, who attacks Harry Potter inside the house. Hermione Granger rescues Harry Potter, but Harry's holly wand is destroyed during their escape. Hermione Granger also discovers evidence connecting Albus Dumbledore to Gellert Grindelwald during their youth.

While guarding the campsite, Harry Potter follows a mysterious silver doe Patronus into the Forest of Dean. The Patronus leads Harry Potter to a frozen pond containing the Sword of Godric Gryffindor. Harry Potter dives into the pond while wearing Salazar Slytherin's locket. The Horcrux attempts to strangle Harry Potter in self-defense. Ron Weasley unexpectedly returns after being guided back by the Deluminator that Albus Dumbledore left him. Ron Weasley rescues Harry Potter, retrieves the Sword of Gryffindor, and uses it to destroy Salazar Slytherin's locket. Before its destruction, the Horcrux creates terrifying illusions that exploit Ron Weasley's deepest fears, including visions of Harry Potter and Hermione Granger rejecting him and sharing a romantic relationship. Ron Weasley overcomes these psychological attacks and destroys the Horcrux, proving his loyalty and personal growth. Hermione Granger initially reacts with anger toward Ron Weasley because he abandoned them, but Ron explains that the Deluminator led him back when Harry Potter most needed help. The reconciliation restores the unity of the trio.

Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger visit Xenophilius Lovegood to learn the meaning of the mysterious symbol. Xenophilius Lovegood explains the legend of the Deathly Hallows through The Tale of the Three Brothers. The Deathly Hallows consist of the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Cloak of Invisibility. According to the legend, the person who possesses all three Hallows becomes the Master of Death. This revelation introduces a second quest that intersects with the Horcrux hunt and explains the significance of several objects connected to Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter.

Xenophilius Lovegood secretly betrays Harry Potter because the Death Eaters have kidnapped his daughter, Luna Lovegood. Xenophilius intentionally speaks Voldemort's name, triggering the magical Taboo that reveals their location. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger escape before the Death Eaters arrive, but Snatchers later capture them after Hermione Granger disguises Harry Potter's appearance with a Stinging Jinx.

During Voldemort's parallel search for ultimate power, Lord Voldemort interrogates the foreign wandmaker Gregorovitch about the Elder Wand before murdering him. Lord Voldemort later captures Gellert Grindelwald at Nurmengard Prison. Grindelwald reveals that Albus Dumbledore possessed the Elder Wand before Voldemort kills him and continues his search.

The Snatchers deliver Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger to Malfoy Manor. Draco Malfoy hesitates when asked to identify Harry Potter, demonstrating growing uncertainty about serving Voldemort. Bellatrix Lestrange notices the Sword of Godric Gryffindor and becomes alarmed because she believes the sword should be secured inside her Gringotts vault. Bellatrix Lestrange imprisons Harry Potter and Ron Weasley alongside Luna Lovegood, Garrick Ollivander, and Griphook the Goblin. Bellatrix Lestrange tortures Hermione Granger in an attempt to discover how the trio acquired the sword.

Harry Potter uses the magical two-way mirror that Sirius Black previously gave him and silently asks for help. Dobby the house-elf responds by Apparating into Malfoy Manor. Dobby rescues Luna Lovegood, Garrick Ollivander, and Griphook before returning to free Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger. Harry Potter disarms Draco Malfoy during the escape, unknowingly transferring Draco's wand allegiance. Bellatrix Lestrange throws a silver dagger as the prisoners Apparate away. The dagger fatally wounds Dobby.

Dobby dies at Shell Cottage after successfully completing the rescue mission. Harry Potter personally digs Dobby's grave without using magic as an act of respect and gratitude. Harry Potter buries Dobby beneath a headstone identifying him as "Here Lies Dobby, A Free Elf." Dobby's death becomes one of the most emotionally significant losses of the war and reinforces the cost of resisting Voldemort.

The events conclude with Lord Voldemort breaking into Albus Dumbledore's tomb. Lord Voldemort removes the Elder Wand from Dumbledore's hands and claims it as his own. Voldemort believes possession of the Elder Wand will eliminate the wand-connection problem that prevented him from killing Harry Potter. This act completes Voldemort's acquisition of the most powerful wand in wizarding history while simultaneously setting the stage for the final confrontation between Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort.

The long-term consequences of these events permanently transform the Second Wizarding War. The Ministry of Magic falls completely under Death Eater control. Hogwarts becomes controlled by Voldemort's regime. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger become fugitives instead of students. One Horcrux, Salazar Slytherin's locket, is destroyed, reducing Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes. The legend of the Deathly Hallows becomes central to understanding the final conflict. Harry Potter gains critical knowledge about Regulus Arcturus Black, Albus Dumbledore's hidden past, Goblin-made magic, and the true nature of the Elder Wand. Ron Weasley overcomes the emotional manipulation of a Horcrux and reaffirms his loyalty to Harry Potter and Hermione Granger. Dobby sacrifices his life to save Harry Potter and his allies. Lord Voldemort acquires the Elder Wand, believing it guarantees victory, while Harry Potter unknowingly gains mastery over Draco Malfoy's wand through disarmament. These interconnected events directly prepare every major participant for the Battle of Hogwarts and the conclusion of the Second Wizarding War.
