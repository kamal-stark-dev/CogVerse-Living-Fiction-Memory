Zhen is a female corsac fox who serves as the deuteragonist of Kung Fu Panda 4. Zhen is initially introduced as a wanted criminal and professional thief before eventually becoming the successor to Po as the new Dragon Warrior. She is also known by the title Dragon Warrior Zhen after Po appoints her as his successor. Zhen is alive and resides at the Jade Palace in the Valley of Peace by the end of the film. Before becoming the Dragon Warrior, Zhen lived on the streets of Juniper City, stayed with the Den of Thieves, lived inside the Chameleon's Fortress, and survived primarily through theft and agility. Zhen specializes in hand-to-hand combat, stealth, acrobatics, infiltration, lockpicking, and cat burglary. She wears a hooded navy-blue tunic, loose brown trousers, a black belt, gray bandages, and a jadeite piercing on her right ear that also functions as a lockpick.

Zhen's biological parents are unknown and are deceased or otherwise absent from her life. Zhen grew up as an orphan and never knew a stable family during her early childhood. Living alone on the streets of Juniper City forced Zhen to become resourceful, independent, and highly skilled at stealing in order to survive. Her difficult upbringing shaped both her worldview and her belief that trust was dangerous and survival depended on deception.

During her childhood, Zhen was temporarily adopted by Han and the Den of Thieves. Han became Zhen's first mentor and introduced her to organized thievery. Under Han's guidance, Zhen learned survival techniques, criminal skills, and the customs of the Den of Thieves. Although Han accepted Zhen into his organization, this relationship eventually ended when Zhen's path diverged from the Den.

At a later point in her childhood, Zhen encountered the Chameleon. Recognizing Zhen's exceptional natural talent as a thief, the Chameleon took Zhen away from her previous life and effectively became her adoptive mother. The Chameleon trained Zhen in advanced theft, deception, infiltration, and combat. The Chameleon also taught Zhen an unforgiving philosophy centered on survival. One of the Chameleon's defining lessons was that "nobody cares about your feelings." This harsh doctrine became one of the fundamental rules by which Zhen lived for many years. Although Zhen became fiercely loyal to the Chameleon, their relationship was based primarily on manipulation rather than genuine parental affection.

Before the main events of Kung Fu Panda 4, Po had already become the Dragon Warrior and was preparing to succeed Master Oogway as the new spiritual leader of the Valley of Peace. This transition required Po to identify and train a worthy successor to inherit the title of Dragon Warrior. At this point, neither Po nor Zhen realized that their destinies would become intertwined.

Zhen first appears inside the Jade Palace while attempting to steal valuable relics from the Hall of Heroes. During this burglary, Po confronts her and defeats her in combat. Although Zhen fails to escape with the relics, she deliberately leaves Po with a clue concerning Tai Lung. This clue begins Po's investigation into mysterious events involving the apparent return of former villains.

When Po later questions Zhen about Tai Lung, Zhen explains that the individual Po encountered was not actually Tai Lung but the Chameleon disguised through shapeshifting magic. Zhen reveals that the Chameleon is a powerful sorceress capable of assuming the appearance of anyone. This revelation establishes the Chameleon as the true threat facing the Valley of Peace.

Po asks Zhen to guide him to the Chameleon's location. In exchange, Po promises to try to reduce Zhen's prison sentence. This agreement marks the beginning of their reluctant partnership. Before they can leave, Master Shifu interrupts to ask Po about his search for a Dragon Warrior successor. During this conversation, Zhen mistakenly identifies Shifu as a squirrel before Shifu corrects her by explaining that he is a red panda. Po temporarily releases Zhen from custody. Demonstrating her cleverness, Zhen secretly attaches one handcuff to both a goose guard and Shifu, preventing Shifu from immediately stopping their departure.

While traveling toward Juniper City, Zhen asks Po for food. Po willingly shares half of one of his cookies with her. This seemingly minor interaction begins establishing mutual trust between the two characters. Along their journey, they stop at the Happy Bunny Tavern for a meal.

Inside the Happy Bunny Tavern, Zhen participates in a gambling game with Granny Boar while Po hires Captain Fish to transport them to Juniper City. Zhen pays for their meal after winning the gambling game. However, she accidentally reveals that she cheated against Granny Boar, forcing Po to unsuccessfully calm the angry customer. The incident highlights the contrast between Po's honesty and Zhen's habitual reliance on deception.

After leaving the tavern, Po and Zhen accidentally fall but are rescued by Captain Fish's boat. During their voyage to Juniper City, Zhen reveals an important part of her past by telling Po that she was an orphan just like him. She explains that stealing was never a personal ambition but rather the only means she had to survive. This shared experience strengthens Po's empathy toward her and begins changing his perception of Zhen from criminal to victim of circumstance.

Upon arriving in Juniper City, Po discovers that Zhen remains a wanted criminal throughout the city. Bull guards quickly identify both of them. Although Po attempts to resolve the situation peacefully, both he and Zhen are arrested. Zhen demonstrates her exceptional lockpicking abilities by unlocking a hidden door disguised as a drum, allowing both of them to escape into the underground Den of Thieves.

Inside the Den of Thieves, Zhen reunites with Han after a long separation. Although Han once cared for Zhen, he remains suspicious of her return. Zhen introduces Po as the Dragon Warrior, but Han and the other thieves have never heard of Po or his accomplishments. Po attempts to inspire the thieves through peaceful speeches and proverbs, but his efforts only confuse them. Zhen openly criticizes Po's attempts, humorously suggesting that he should improve his proverbs. Despite his skepticism, Han eventually agrees to help them while warning that he will personally turn Zhen over to the authorities if she and Po remain after nightfall.

The following day, Han's assistance enables Po and Zhen to infiltrate the Chameleon's palace. After bypassing the iguana guards, Po becomes trapped inside a cage. Zhen suggests that Po hand her the Staff of Wisdom so she can use it to free him. Instead, Zhen steps backward and reluctantly delivers the Staff of Wisdom directly to the Chameleon. The Chameleon reveals that Zhen had been working for her all along and that stealing the Staff of Wisdom had always been Zhen's assigned mission. The Chameleon explains that the Staff of Wisdom will allow access to the Spirit Realm so she can summon dead kung fu masters and steal their kung fu abilities.

Zhen's betrayal deeply hurts Po because he had begun trusting her despite her criminal past. However, Zhen immediately begins regretting her decision. Her loyalty to the Chameleon conflicts with everything she has recently learned from Po about compassion, forgiveness, and personal growth.

After Po escapes imprisonment and confronts the Chameleon again, the Chameleon apparently kills Po by knocking him into a great abyss. Zhen is horrified because the Chameleon had previously promised not to harm Po. When Zhen reminds the Chameleon of this promise, the Chameleon coldly reveals that she never cared about Zhen's emotions and repeats the harsh lesson she had always taught: "Nobody cares about your feelings." This revelation completely destroys Zhen's loyalty. For the first time, Zhen realizes that the Chameleon never truly loved or valued her and had only manipulated her as a useful tool.

The Chameleon subsequently uses the Staff of Wisdom to summon numerous deceased kung fu masters and villains from the Spirit Realm, including Tai Lung, Lord Shen, and General Kai. The Chameleon steals their kung fu abilities in order to make herself nearly unstoppable. Witnessing the Chameleon's betrayal and abuse of these legendary warriors convinces Zhen to abandon her former adoptive mother permanently.

Zhen discovers that Po survived his fall. When she attempts to apologize, Po initially rejects her apology and admits that trusting her had been a mistake. During an emotional confrontation, Zhen first appears ready to attack Po in frustration but instead embraces him in a heartfelt hug. Through this action, Zhen demonstrates that she genuinely cares about Po's safety rather than simply using him. She warns Po that the Chameleon has become even more dangerous after stealing the abilities of numerous kung fu masters.

Although Po decides to face the Chameleon alone to avoid placing Zhen in danger, Zhen refuses to abandon him. She joins forces with Mr. Ping and Li Shan to gather an army from the Den of Thieves. Together they organize the thieves into a resistance force capable of delaying the Chameleon's army while Zhen returns to assist Po directly inside the palace.

During the final battle, Po once again becomes trapped inside a cage. Unlike their previous encounter, Po now fully trusts Zhen. He deliberately entrusts the Staff of Wisdom to her and instructs her to confront the Chameleon herself. This decision demonstrates Po's complete faith in Zhen's capacity for redemption and growth.

Armed with the Staff of Wisdom, Zhen battles the Chameleon directly. Using the combat abilities she learned throughout her life together with the confidence Po inspired in her, Zhen successfully overpowers the Chameleon. Her victory provides Po with the opportunity to escape captivity.

As the Chameleon launches a final attack, Po counters using the Staff of Wisdom and permanently defeats her. Po restores the stolen kung fu abilities to every master imprisoned by the Chameleon and releases all the spirits she had captured.

After their freedom is restored, Tai Lung publicly acknowledges that Po truly deserves the title of Dragon Warrior. Lord Shen, General Kai, and the other restored masters likewise bow respectfully to Po. Tai Lung asks Po to reopen the portal to the Spirit Realm so the former villains can return voluntarily. Before leaving, Tai Lung captures the Chameleon to take responsibility for punishing her within the Spirit Realm.

The defeated Chameleon desperately begs Zhen for help. Zhen refuses and repeats the Chameleon's own lesson by reminding her that "nobody cares about your feelings." By returning the Chameleon's own philosophy against her, Zhen symbolically rejects everything the Chameleon taught her and fully embraces her new moral values.

After returning to the Valley of Peace, Zhen wonders what kind of future she should pursue after serving her sentence. She expresses interest in studying acupuncture, demonstrating her desire to contribute positively to society instead of returning to crime. Po instead offers her a far greater opportunity by naming her as his chosen successor and the next Dragon Warrior.

Zhen initially questions whether she deserves such an honor because of her criminal past. Po reassures her by explaining that extraordinary outcomes often arise from the most unlikely beginnings. Although amused by Po's growing collection of food-themed proverbs, Zhen eagerly asks when her actual training will begin, demonstrating genuine enthusiasm for her new life.

After receiving parole, Zhen officially moves into the Jade Palace. Po begins training her personally while Master Shifu and the Furious Five also become her instructors. This marks Zhen's complete transition from criminal apprentice to legitimate kung fu student.

Viper evaluates Zhen's speed and reflexes inside the Field of Fiery Death, where Zhen performs well despite suffering minor burns. Tigress spars with Zhen on the Jade Tortoise of Wisdom, though Zhen ultimately loses her footing and falls. Crane teaches Zhen advanced techniques for deflecting incoming spears. Mantis trains Zhen within the Gauntlet of Wooden Warriors. Monkey effortlessly demonstrates movement across the Seven-Talon Rings before Zhen attempts the exercise herself and becomes stuck, illustrating that her training has only begun.

Throughout her life, Zhen evolves from an abandoned orphan into a street thief, from loyal servant of the Chameleon into a conflicted ally of Po, and finally into the Dragon Warrior chosen to protect the Valley of Peace. Her relationships with Han and the Chameleon teach her survival and mistrust, while her relationship with Po teaches forgiveness, self-worth, trust, and redemption. By rejecting the Chameleon's philosophy and accepting responsibility for her own choices, Zhen proves that personal history does not determine personal destiny. Her appointment as the new Dragon Warrior establishes her as the future protector of the Valley of Peace and the next generation of the Jade Palace's kung fu legacy.
