Li Shan, commonly known as Li, is a giant panda who is the biological father of Po and a major supporting character in the Kung Fu Panda franchise. He has also been referred to as Panda Dad, particularly in the credits of *Kung Fu Panda 2*. During one misunderstanding, Mr. Ping mistakenly identified Li as a monster before learning his true identity. Li is a male giant panda with green eyes and black, white, and grey fur. Throughout different periods of his life he wears different clothing, including a green cloak and beige pants during the events surrounding *Kung Fu Panda 2*, a green vest with green pants and a brown belt during *Kung Fu Panda 3*, and later a light green vest with purple pants and a brown belt during *Kung Fu Panda 4*. When traveling, Li commonly wears a straw hat.

Li originally lived in the Old Panda Village with his wife and their infant son, Po. He worked as a farmer and enjoyed a peaceful family life before tragedy struck. His immediate family consisted of his wife, whose name is unknown and who later died, and his son Po. Li is also related to the pandas Dim and Sum, who are identified as his nephews, although they may instead be related through his wife. After reuniting with Po later in life, Li eventually became co-owner of Dragon Warrior Noodles and Tofu alongside his family. He later became a kung fu student under Po and learned to manipulate chi.

Approximately twenty years before the events of *Kung Fu Panda 2*, Li lived happily with his family in the panda village. During this period, Lord Shen, an ambitious peacock, launched a massacre against the panda population with the assistance of his wolf army. The invasion completely changed Li's life. When Boss Wolf and another wolf attempted to kill the infant Po, Li intervened to defend his son. Using a farming rake as a weapon, Li struck back against the attackers, driving them away. During the confrontation, Li permanently damaged Boss Wolf's left eye, leaving the wolf scarred for life. The resistance surprised Lord Shen, who reacted with anger after witnessing Li's defense of his family.

Recognizing that the village could not be saved, Li instructed his wife to flee with their son while he held back the attackers. His wife obeyed and escaped with Po. The exact events that followed remain unknown, but Li survived the massacre while becoming permanently separated from both his wife and his son. His wife ultimately died after ensuring Po escaped from Lord Shen's forces. Believing that his family had been lost, Li fled alongside the remaining panda survivors. Together they established a hidden Panda Village deep within the mountains, where they remained concealed from the outside world for many years. The destruction of the original panda village and the loss of his family became the defining trauma of Li's life and heavily influenced his future decisions.

During the years of isolation, Li remained among the surviving pandas in the hidden Panda Village. Although separated from Po, he never abandoned hope entirely. At the conclusion of the events surrounding Lord Shen's defeat, Grand Master Oogway sent Li what Li described as a message from the universe. This message revealed that Po was still alive. Upon learning this truth, Li joyfully declared that his son had survived. This revelation ended decades of uncertainty and motivated Li to leave the safety of the hidden village in search of Po.

When Li eventually arrived at the Valley of Peace during the events of *Kung Fu Panda 3*, he unknowingly encountered Po in Mr. Ping's noodle shop. Li was participating in a dumpling-eating contest and even surpassed Po's previous dumpling-eating record, demonstrating personality traits remarkably similar to his son's love of food. When Po asked who he was, Li explained that he was searching for the son he had lost many years earlier. Po responded that he himself had lost his father. Despite the obvious connection, neither Li nor Po immediately realized they were speaking to each other. After departing, both recognized the truth and reunited emotionally. Their reunion ended nearly two decades of separation and restored their biological father-son relationship.

After reuniting, Li explained that other giant pandas had survived Lord Shen's massacre by living in a hidden village. He also explained that he had learned Po was alive through a message from the universe. Mr. Ping, who had raised Po from infancy after finding him abandoned, viewed Li's explanation with skepticism and struggled emotionally with the return of Po's biological father. Despite this initial tension, both fathers deeply loved Po and ultimately developed mutual respect based upon their shared desire to protect him.

Following the reunion, Po introduced Li to the Jade Palace. Father and son spent time together exploring the Hall of Warriors, where they playfully handled legendary kung fu weapons and artifacts. Their shared enthusiasm highlighted the many similarities between their personalities. Shortly afterward, Kai's jade warriors attacked the Valley of Peace. The attack introduced Li to the growing threat posed by Kai, a former ally of Oogway who had mastered the theft of chi. During discussions about Kai's history, the heroes concluded that Po needed to master chi in order to defeat him. Li volunteered to teach Po because ancient panda traditions were historically connected with chi.

Li persuaded Po to travel to the hidden Panda Village, where Po could reconnect with his heritage. Mr. Ping chose to accompany them despite his concerns regarding his place in Po's life. Upon arriving, Po experienced a powerful sense of belonging after meeting the surviving panda community. Li attempted to teach Po what it meant to live as a panda through ordinary cultural experiences such as eating together, swimming, rolling, relaxing, and spending time with family. These activities reflected Li's belief that panda identity was expressed through community, joy, and everyday life rather than solely through martial arts.

Although Li claimed he could teach Po the ancient art of chi, this claim was false. In reality, the ancient techniques had been lost over generations, and Li possessed no knowledge of how to perform them. Li deliberately concealed this truth because he feared losing Po a second time. His deception was motivated by parental love rather than selfish ambition. Li believed that convincing Po to remain safely within the hidden Panda Village would protect him from Kai's approaching invasion.

After Tigress arrived to warn the pandas that Kai was approaching, Li finally admitted that he had lied. The confession devastated Po, who felt betrayed by the father he had only recently regained. Already burdened by his responsibilities as the Dragon Warrior, Po denounced Li as his father and chose to confront Kai alone using the Wuxi Finger Hold. Tigress informed Po that Kai could not be defeated through solitary action. Li's deception temporarily damaged his relationship with Po, demonstrating how fear of loss had clouded his judgment.

Following a heartfelt conversation between Li and Mr. Ping, the two fathers recognized that protecting Po did not mean preventing him from fulfilling his destiny. Inspired by this realization, Li encouraged the panda villagers to train under Po instead of hiding from danger. Li personally persuaded the previously peaceful pandas to prepare for battle. This marked an important stage in Li's personal growth, as he transformed from someone motivated primarily by fear into someone willing to stand beside his son despite the risks.

Under Po's unconventional instruction, Li and the other pandas learned to work together in combat. Although Li primarily practiced a defensive style of kung fu rather than aggressive techniques, he actively participated in defending the Panda Village against Kai's jade zombies, also known as jombies. The panda defenders successfully disrupted Kai's forces long enough for Po to confront Kai directly.

During the final battle, Po realized that Kai could not be defeated through ordinary combat. Po performed the Wuxi Finger Hold on himself and Kai simultaneously, transporting both of them to the Spirit Realm. Po's sacrifice protected Li, the panda village, and everyone else in the Mortal Realm. Witnessing Po willingly sacrifice himself inspired Li and the surviving pandas to rediscover the forgotten principles of chi. Li became the first panda to imitate the chi technique described on the ancient scroll. He encouraged the remaining pandas to contribute their own chi as well. Together, the pandas transferred their collective chi to Po. This act enabled Po to overpower Kai, destroy him permanently, master chi, and return safely to the Mortal Realm. Li later declared that Po had taught him who he was meant to be: a father. This realization represented the culmination of Li's emotional journey from grieving survivor to supportive parent.

After Kai's defeat, Li, Mr. Ping, Po, the pandas, and the residents of the Valley of Peace celebrated together. The widespread use of chi manipulation demonstrated that the ancient panda legacy had been restored. The victory also reunited Po's two fathers within a single extended family, replacing earlier jealousy with mutual affection and cooperation. Li permanently relocated to the Valley of Peace, where he remained close to Po and continued supporting him.

During later events, Li became co-owner of Dragon Warrior Noodles and Tofu. His residence changed over the course of his life from the Old Panda Village to the hidden Panda Village and ultimately to the Valley of Peace. His occupations evolved from farmer to kung fu student and later business co-owner, reflecting the major transitions in his life after reuniting with Po.

Li practices a primarily defensive style of kung fu. Although never portrayed as a great martial arts master, Li consistently demonstrates courage, resilience, and a willingness to place himself in danger for his family. His greatest combat achievement during the panda massacre was protecting the infant Po and permanently injuring Boss Wolf. During Kai's invasion, Li fought alongside the panda defenders and helped resist the jade zombie army. His most significant ability is chi manipulation, which he learned through Po's guidance during the final battle against Kai. In later continuity, including *Kung Fu Panda: The Paws of Destiny*, Li is also associated with Guardian Chi.

Li's defining possessions include the farming rake that he used while defending his family during Lord Shen's attack and an altar containing a painting of his late wife and infant Po. The altar demonstrates that Li continued honoring his wife throughout the decades following her death and never stopped grieving the loss of his family.

Li's personality combines warmth, optimism, and emotional depth. He is naturally laid-back, playful, food-loving, sociable, and fond of eating, sleeping, and enjoying simple pleasures. His carefree lifestyle strongly resembles Po's personality before Po fully embraced kung fu. Li enjoys bonding through shared experiences rather than formal instruction and believes family relationships are strengthened through everyday companionship. Despite these easygoing qualities, Li possesses remarkable courage when protecting those he loves. He willingly risked his life during Lord Shen's massacre to defend his wife and infant son. His long separation from Po left him emotionally scarred and fearful of experiencing another loss. This fear explains why he deceived Po regarding his knowledge of chi. Although the deception caused temporary conflict, Li accepted responsibility for his mistake and ultimately supported Po's destiny rather than attempting to prevent it.

Li's relationships define much of his life. His marriage to Po's mother was loving and supportive until her death during Lord Shen's campaign against the pandas. His relationship with Po evolved from devoted father during Po's infancy, to decades of painful separation, to joyful reunion, temporary conflict caused by deception, and finally a mature partnership founded on trust, pride, and mutual respect. Li's relationship with Mr. Ping initially contained tension because both considered themselves Po's father, but this evolved into friendship and cooperation after both recognized that Po needed them both. Li's principal enemy is Lord Shen, whose massacre destroyed Li's original home and family. Kai later became another enemy because Kai threatened both Po and the surviving pandas. Li also opposed Boss Wolf after defending Po during the panda massacre. Li later studied under Po, making Po both his son and his kung fu master.

Li's life demonstrates the enduring importance of family, cultural identity, forgiveness, and perseverance. His survival preserved the remaining panda community after Lord Shen's genocide. His reunion with Po restored an entire family that had been broken for decades. His willingness to admit his mistakes strengthened his relationship with Po rather than destroying it. His leadership encouraged the peaceful pandas to defend their home, and his rediscovery of collective chi directly enabled Kai's defeat. Through these actions, Li helped restore the lost traditions of the giant pandas and ensured that their legacy continued into the future.
