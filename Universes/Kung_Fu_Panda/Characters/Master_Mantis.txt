Master Mantis

Master Mantis, also known as Master Mantis and "Green Baby," is a male Chinese mantis and one of the supporting protagonists of the Kung Fu Panda franchise. Master Mantis is a master of Mantis Style kung fu, also known as Praying Mantis kung fu, and is one of the five elite warriors known collectively as the Furious Five. Master Mantis trained under Master Shifu at the Jade Palace in the Valley of Peace and later acknowledged Po, the Dragon Warrior, as one of his current masters. In addition to being a kung fu master, Master Mantis also serves as a doctor and an accomplished acupuncturist. Master Mantis later becomes an ally of Zhen following her succession as the Dragon Warrior.

Master Mantis is a living Chinese mantis with yellow eyes, red irises, green skin, and teal, yellow, and black markings across his back. Although he is physically the smallest member of the Furious Five, he possesses extraordinary speed, remarkable physical strength for his size, exceptional agility, and mastery of precise martial arts techniques.

Master Mantis was born in Mantis' Village to an unnamed mother and an unnamed father. His father later died. At some point in adulthood, Master Mantis married an unnamed wife. From an early age, Mantis possessed a fast-paced personality and believed that the rest of the world moved too slowly to keep up with him. His impatience, confidence, and tendency to act before listening frequently placed him in dangerous situations.

During his youth, Mantis accepted a mission to recover stolen wool coats from the Wool Stealing Crocodile Bandits. A villager attempted to warn him that the bandits had prepared traps, but Mantis ignored the warning because of his overconfidence. As a direct consequence of refusing to listen, he was captured by the crocodile bandits and imprisoned inside a cage.

The crocodile bandits forced Mantis to remain confined for many days with nothing to do except wait. The experience fundamentally changed him. During his imprisonment, Mantis entered a meditative trance that altered his perception of time. Instead of believing the world moved too slowly, he came to perceive the world as moving faster than himself. This new perspective taught him patience, observation, and strategic thinking. Rather than relying solely upon speed, Mantis carefully devised an escape plan. He pretended to be dead until the crocodile bandits opened his cage, allowing him to surprise them, defeat them, and recover his freedom. This experience transformed impatience into discipline and became the foundation of his later martial philosophy.

Following his personal growth, Master Mantis eventually trained under Master Shifu at the Jade Palace. Through disciplined instruction, he mastered Mantis Style kung fu, developing extraordinary speed, precision, reflexes, balance, and explosive striking power. Despite his tiny physical stature, he became one of the most formidable martial artists in China.

Master Mantis later joined Tigress, Monkey, Viper, and Crane to form the Furious Five, an elite team of kung fu masters responsible for defending the Valley of Peace. Although physically the smallest member of the team, Mantis consistently proved capable of fighting opponents many times larger than himself through technique, intelligence, and determination.

Before the events of the first film, Master Mantis regularly trained alongside the Furious Five under Master Shifu's supervision. During one training exercise, the Furious Five attempted to ambush Shifu together. Although their combined attack failed, the exercise demonstrated Mantis' continued dedication to improving his martial abilities under his master's instruction.

After Master Oogway foresaw the return of Tai Lung, the Dragon Warrior Tournament was organized to determine who would inherit the Dragon Scroll. Master Mantis participated alongside the other members of the Furious Five, although his individual tournament demonstration was not shown. When Master Oogway sensed that the Dragon Warrior had arrived, Mantis assembled with Tigress, Monkey, Crane, and Viper for the final selection.

As Master Oogway prepared to choose the Dragon Warrior, Po unexpectedly fell into the arena directly in front of Oogway. Oogway interpreted this event as a sign from the universe and proclaimed Po to be the Dragon Warrior. Mantis, together with the rest of the Furious Five, Master Shifu, the assembled villagers, and even Po himself, was astonished by the unexpected selection.

Following the tournament, Master Mantis resumed training inside the Training Hall, where he practiced within the Gauntlet of Wooden Warriors. When Po arrived to begin his own kung fu training, Mantis was initially unimpressed by the inexperienced panda. He watched as Po attempted to spar with the Adversary training device before being thrown into the obstacle course, where Po failed every challenge, including the Gauntlet of Wooden Warriors that Mantis himself had mastered.

Later that evening, Mantis joined Monkey, Crane, Viper, and Tigress in joking about Po's inability to perform kung fu while walking toward the student barracks. Neither Mantis nor the others realized that Po was secretly following behind them and overheard every critical remark.

The following day, Mantis expected that Po had abandoned his training. Instead, he discovered that Po had awakened early and had continued practicing on his own despite becoming stuck during the process. Throughout that day's training, several members of the Furious Five sparred with Po, with Mantis becoming the final teammate to face him before Master Shifu personally intervened. Mantis watched as Shifu repeatedly defeated Po before ultimately throwing him out of the Jade Palace.

Although Mantis continued making occasional jokes about Po's clumsy movements, he became one of the first members of the Furious Five to recognize Po's extraordinary determination. Unlike Tigress, Mantis realized that Po willingly endured humiliation because he genuinely wished to become a better person. His respect for Po steadily increased as he witnessed the panda refuse to give up despite repeated failures.

When Tai Lung escaped from prison and advanced toward the Valley of Peace, the Furious Five confronted him on the Thread of Hope, a bridge located just outside the Valley. During the battle, Mantis demonstrated his extraordinary physical strength by supporting the collapsing bridge while the remaining members of the Furious Five continued fighting Tai Lung. His actions prevented his teammates from falling and allowed the battle to continue.

Despite the combined efforts of the Furious Five, Tai Lung ultimately overwhelmed them. Tai Lung used his nerve-strike technique to disable Tigress, Monkey, Viper, and Mantis through chi-blocking attacks. Crane was intentionally left unharmed so that he could carry his injured teammates back to the Jade Palace and deliver news of Tai Lung's approach. The battle represented one of Mantis' greatest defeats, but it also demonstrated his unwavering loyalty to his teammates and his willingness to sacrifice himself for their safety.

While Po briefly departed to reunite with Mr. Ping, Mantis assisted with evacuating the civilians of the Valley of Peace. He received responsibility for gathering the northern farmers and successfully guided them toward safety before joining the rest of the evacuation effort.

During the evacuation, an enormous golden wave of energy spread across the Valley following Po's battle with Tai Lung. Mantis and the other evacuees returned to investigate and discovered that Po had defeated Tai Lung using the legendary Wuxi Finger Hold. Witnessing Po accomplish what the Furious Five had failed to achieve convinced Mantis that Master Oogway's decision had been correct. Led by Tigress, Mantis joined the Furious Five and the citizens of the Valley of Peace in respectfully bowing before Po, acknowledging him as both a true kung fu master and the rightful Dragon Warrior.

Following Tai Lung's defeat, Master Mantis developed a close friendship with Po. Mantis became one of the earliest members of the Furious Five to openly accept and appreciate Po as both a person and a martial artist. Rather than judging warriors by outward appearance, Mantis came to believe that determination, heart, and intelligence mattered far more than physical size or conventional expectations.

Master Mantis possesses a personality that combines confidence, practicality, humor, and directness. Although physically tiny, he refuses to allow others to define him by his size. He is particularly sensitive whenever others mock or underestimate him because of his stature. This sensitivity reflects years of overcoming assumptions that smaller fighters cannot become great warriors.

Master Mantis firmly believes that physical size does not determine martial ability. His own accomplishments demonstrate this philosophy, and he later recognized the same principle in Po. Mantis concluded that Po's unusually large body could become an advantage rather than a weakness, just as Mantis himself had transformed his own small size into a strength. This belief became one of the strongest foundations of his friendship with Po.

Among the members of the Furious Five, Master Mantis is one of the most outspoken and expressive. He frequently delivers dry observations and witty remarks even during tense situations. His humor often serves to reduce tension among his companions while reflecting his practical outlook on life. Although he enjoys making jokes, he consistently demonstrates courage, loyalty, and professionalism whenever lives are endangered.

Master Mantis is also an accomplished physician and acupuncturist. He regularly treats injured companions using acupuncture techniques and combines medical knowledge with martial arts expertise. His willingness to care for wounded allies demonstrates that his commitment to protecting others extends beyond combat.

Master Mantis' personal development centers upon transforming impatience into discipline. As a young warrior, he believed speed alone could solve every problem, and that belief led directly to his capture by the Wool Stealing Crocodile Bandits. His imprisonment forced him to learn patience, observation, and careful planning. Those lessons allowed him to mature into both an exceptional martial artist and a dependable teammate. Later, his acceptance of Po reinforced his belief that true strength comes from determination and character rather than appearance.

Master Mantis remains one of the foremost practitioners of Mantis Style kung fu, a respected member of the Furious Five, a trusted ally of Po and Zhen, a lifelong student of Master Shifu, and one of the principal defenders of the Valley of Peace. His legacy is defined by proving that patience can be learned through adversity, that physical size never limits true strength, and that wisdom often comes from overcoming one's own greatest flaws.
