The Sokovia Accords were an international political response to the destruction caused by superhuman battles, especially the Battle of Sokovia and other Avengers operations. The Accords were designed to place the Avengers and other enhanced individuals under government supervision so that their actions could be controlled and authorized by the United Nations. The agreement turned the Avengers from loosely independent heroes into a politically contested force.

The main conflict around the Sokovia Accords centered on Tony Stark and Steve Rogers, who disagreed sharply about accountability, freedom, and oversight. Tony Stark believed supervision was necessary because unchecked power had caused enormous damage. Steve Rogers believed that handing heroism over to governments could create new forms of abuse and delay people from doing the right thing. Their disagreement split the Avengers into opposing factions.

The Sokovia Accords led directly to the Avengers Civil War and reshaped the MCU's political landscape. The Accords did not solve the problem of superhuman conflict, but they did expose the tension between security and autonomy. The event remains a major example of how the MCU links heroism to international consequence.
