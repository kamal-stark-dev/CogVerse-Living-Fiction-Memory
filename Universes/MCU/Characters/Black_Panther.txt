T'Challa, known as Black Panther, is the king of Wakanda and one of the MCU's most important leaders. T'Challa inherits both the throne and the mantle of the Black Panther after the death of his father, King T'Chaka. T'Challa is defined by honor, restraint, intelligence, and a strong commitment to Wakanda's people.

T'Challa is closely connected to Shuri, Nakia, Okoye, and the broader Wakandan royal and military structure. His conflict with Erik Killmonger forces T'Challa to confront Wakanda's isolationist policies and to redefine what leadership means. T'Challa later becomes an Avenger and helps defend Earth against Thanos during the battle in Wakanda.

T'Challa's reign expands Wakanda's role in the world. Instead of remaining hidden, Wakanda begins to engage more openly with global problems under his leadership. T'Challa's death later marks a major turning point for both Wakanda and the MCU, but his legacy continues through the kingdom, his family, and the symbolic role of the Black Panther.
