Dobby was a male British house-elf who was born on 28 June before 1973. He belonged to the species known as house-elves and stood approximately three feet to three and a half feet tall. Dobby had brown skin, large green eyes resembling tennis balls, a long pencil-like nose, and large pointed bat-like ears. His boggart took the form of Lord Voldemort, indicating that Voldemort represented Dobby's greatest fear.

For much of his life, Dobby served the Malfoy family as their house-elf. His masters were Dark wizards who treated him with cruelty and abuse. During his years of servitude, Dobby was legally bound to obey the Malfoy family, although he possessed an unusually strong conscience that frequently conflicted with his required obedience. Whenever Dobby believed he had disobeyed or dishonoured his masters, he punished himself through painful acts of self-harm, including striking his own head against objects and even ironing his own hands. These punishments reflected the deeply ingrained magical and psychological conditioning placed upon house-elves rather than personal malice toward himself.

While serving the Malfoy family, Dobby became indirectly aware of Lord Voldemort's plans through the communication between Voldemort and the Malfoys. During 1992, Dobby learned that Lucius Malfoy intended to reopen the Chamber of Secrets using Tom Marvolo Riddle's enchanted diary. Understanding that Harry Potter would be placed in mortal danger if he returned to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, Dobby deliberately violated his master's interests in order to protect Harry Potter.

Dobby secretly visited Harry Potter before Harry's second year at Hogwarts. Dobby attempted to convince Harry Potter not to return to school because Dobby believed remaining away from Hogwarts would save Harry's life. Harry Potter refused to stay away from Hogwarts because Hogwarts represented Harry's home, education, and friendships. Unable to persuade Harry through words alone, Dobby resorted to increasingly desperate actions intended to force Harry to remain away from school.

Dobby magically levitated a pudding inside the Dursley residence. The Ministry of Magic mistakenly concluded that Harry Potter had cast the Hover Charm, resulting in Harry receiving an official warning for underage magic. Dobby also magically sealed the barrier leading to Platform Nine and Three-Quarters, preventing Harry Potter and Ron Weasley from reaching the Hogwarts Express through normal means.

When Harry Potter nevertheless reached Hogwarts, Dobby magically tampered with a Bludger during a Quidditch match. The enchanted Bludger relentlessly pursued Harry Potter with the intention of seriously injuring him badly enough that he would be sent home from school. Dobby never intended to kill Harry Potter. Dobby believed temporary injury would ultimately save Harry's life by removing him from Lucius Malfoy's larger plot involving the Chamber of Secrets. Although Dobby's intentions were protective, his actions demonstrated poor judgment and a failure to appreciate Harry Potter's emotional attachment to Hogwarts and the importance of Harry's education. Harry Potter later expressed anger after learning Dobby's role in these incidents, but ultimately forgave Dobby after understanding Dobby's motives.

At the conclusion of the 1992–1993 school year, Harry Potter defeated the preserved memory of Tom Marvolo Riddle inside the Chamber of Secrets and killed the Basilisk. After these events, Harry Potter devised a plan to free Dobby from slavery without Lucius Malfoy realizing the deception beforehand.

Harry Potter concealed Tom Riddle's Diary inside one of his own socks before returning the diary to Lucius Malfoy. Lucius Malfoy removed the diary from the sock in disgust and carelessly threw the sock aside. Dobby caught Harry Potter's sock. Because Lucius Malfoy had unintentionally presented clothing to his house-elf, the magical bond of servitude was broken, and Dobby became a free elf. This event permanently changed Dobby's life and became the defining moment of his personal identity.

Immediately after gaining his freedom, Lucius Malfoy attempted to attack Harry Potter in anger. Dobby intervened and used powerful house-elf magic to blast Lucius Malfoy backward, sending him flying down multiple flights of stairs. Dobby openly declared that he was no longer owned by any master and proudly identified himself as a free elf. This confrontation marked the first time Dobby was able to openly oppose his former master without magical restriction.

After obtaining his freedom, Dobby experienced a period of unemployment. Many other house-elves viewed freedom with shame and regarded Dobby's independence as disgraceful. Dobby was frequently ostracised because he did not believe house-elves should remain enslaved. Despite experiencing rejection from many members of his own species, Dobby remained proud of his freedom and never regretted leaving the abusive Malfoy household.

Although Dobby treasured his independence, he genuinely enjoyed working. Dobby believed meaningful work and personal freedom could coexist. Albus Dumbledore offered Dobby employment at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry under fair conditions that respected Dobby's autonomy. Dumbledore initially offered Dobby ten Galleons per week together with weekends off. Dobby considered these terms excessively generous and negotiated for significantly lower compensation because he wished to work rather than become wealthy. Dobby and Albus Dumbledore eventually agreed that Dobby would receive one Galleon each week together with one day off every month.

Beginning in December 1994, Dobby worked in the Hogwarts kitchens. Unlike his previous life under the Malfoys, Dobby could choose his own work, come and go freely, and complete relatively simple responsibilities while maintaining his personal independence. Employment at Hogwarts represented the first happy and stable period of Dobby's life.

After arriving at Hogwarts, Dobby was accompanied by Winky, another house-elf who had recently been dismissed from service. Winky struggled emotionally with her dismissal and frequently became intoxicated by drinking butterbeer. Dobby attempted to help Winky adapt to freedom, found her employment at Hogwarts, and regularly cared for her whenever she became drunk. Their friendship demonstrated Dobby's compassion toward other house-elves despite their differing views regarding freedom.

Dobby developed exceptionally close friendships with Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger. Harry Potter became Dobby's closest friend and the individual whom Dobby admired most. Dobby regarded Harry Potter as his best friend because Harry had liberated him from slavery. Dobby also considered Albus Dumbledore to be his rightful and true master, although Dumbledore treated Dobby as a free individual rather than as property.

Dobby supported Hermione Granger's efforts through the Society for the Promotion of Elfish Welfare (S.P.E.W.), an organization dedicated to improving the rights and treatment of house-elves. Although most Hogwarts house-elves rejected Hermione's attempts to free them by hiding clothing around Gryffindor Tower, Dobby appreciated the gifts. The other house-elves refused to clean Gryffindor Tower because they feared accidental liberation, while Dobby happily collected Hermione's knitted hats, scarves, and socks and proudly wore them.

After becoming free, Dobby abandoned the traditional pillowcase worn by enslaved house-elves. He instead wore colourful, mismatched clothing, including jumpers, shorts, and especially socks. Socks became Dobby's favourite articles of clothing because Harry Potter's sock had granted him freedom. Dobby particularly enjoyed brightly coloured mismatched socks and became confused when receiving identical pairs because he preferred variety. Dobby also enjoyed giving socks as presents to others.

During Christmas 1994, Harry Potter gave Dobby an old pair of mustard-yellow socks. Ron Weasley gave Dobby a pair of violet socks together with a jumper. Following the second task of the Triwizard Tournament, Harry Potter also gave Dobby socks purchased from Gladrags Wizardwear. Dobby treasured these gifts because they symbolised friendship, appreciation, and personal freedom rather than servitude.

Dobby permanently kept the original dirty sock that Harry Potter had unknowingly given him through Lucius Malfoy. Dobby regarded this sock as one of his greatest treasures and continued wearing it until the day of his death. The sock symbolized Dobby's liberation from slavery and Harry Potter's kindness.

Dobby possessed powerful innate house-elf magic that often exceeded conventional wizarding limitations. Dobby could Apparate even within locations that normally prevented Apparition, including Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and Malfoy Manor. This ability made him especially valuable during dangerous rescue missions.

Dobby demonstrated the ability to levitate objects through magic, manipulate enchanted objects, produce powerful magical blasts by snapping his fingers, and disarm witches and wizards without using a wand. His magical abilities reflected the exceptional magical power naturally possessed by house-elves despite the low social status imposed upon them by wizarding society.

In 1997, Dobby assisted Harry Potter by spying on Draco Malfoy. Dobby performed this mission together with Kreacher. This assignment demonstrated that Dobby continued supporting Harry Potter's efforts even after many years had passed since Harry freed him. Dobby also became associated with Dumbledore's Army and the Order of the Phoenix through his continued assistance to Harry Potter and his allies.

In early 1998, Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, and several companions were imprisoned inside Malfoy Manor by Death Eaters. Aberforth Dumbledore ordered Dobby to rescue the prisoners. Dobby Apparated into Malfoy Manor despite its magical protections and successfully transported Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, Ron Weasley, Luna Lovegood, Dean Thomas, Ollivander, and Griphook away from captivity.

During the escape from Malfoy Manor, Bellatrix Lestrange threw a knife toward the escaping prisoners. Although Dobby successfully Apparated Harry Potter and Griphook to safety at Shell Cottage in Tinworth, Cornwall, the knife struck Dobby during the escape. The wound proved fatal.

Dobby died in late March 1998 at Shell Cottage after successfully completing the rescue mission. Dobby's final actions ensured the survival of Harry Potter and his companions. His willingness to sacrifice his own life demonstrated the extraordinary depth of his loyalty, bravery, and devotion to his friends.

Harry Potter chose to bury Dobby without using magic. Harry personally dug Dobby's grave by hand in the garden of Shell Cottage as a mark of respect and gratitude. Harry Potter carved the inscription "HERE LIES DOBBY, A FREE ELF" onto Dobby's headstone. The inscription emphasized that Dobby's greatest achievement and proudest identity was not his service but his freedom.

Bellatrix Lestrange was directly responsible for Dobby's death because her thrown knife inflicted the fatal wound. Bellatrix Lestrange herself was later killed by Molly Weasley during the Battle of Hogwarts. Bellatrix's death served as the eventual vengeance for Dobby's murder.

Dobby's personality combined courage, loyalty, compassion, humility, and an exceptionally strong moral conscience. He willingly endangered himself whenever he believed protecting innocent people required personal sacrifice. Dobby consistently chose friendship and justice over blind obedience, even when doing so caused him physical suffering. Although his judgment was sometimes flawed and his plans occasionally lacked foresight, his intentions were consistently motivated by compassion and the desire to protect others.

Dobby continued to speak in the third person throughout his life and frequently used unusual grammar, reflecting traditional house-elf speech patterns. He affectionately referred to Ron Weasley as Harry Potter's "Wheezy." These distinctive speech habits became one of Dobby's defining personal characteristics.

Dobby's life represented a transformation from enslaved servant to independent individual. His liberation demonstrated that house-elves could choose freedom while retaining dignity, loyalty, and a love of honest work. His enduring friendship with Harry Potter illustrated that genuine loyalty is earned through kindness rather than enforced through ownership. Dobby's final sacrifice at Shell Cottage permanently established him as one of the most selfless allies of Harry Potter and left a lasting legacy as a symbol of freedom, courage, and the inherent worth of every sentient being regardless of social status.
