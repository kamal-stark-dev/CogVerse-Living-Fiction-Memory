Hermione Jean Granger was born on 19 September 1979 in England, Great Britain. Hermione Granger was an English Muggle-born witch born to a Muggle family consisting of Mr Granger and Mrs Granger, who both worked as dentists. Hermione Granger belonged to the Granger family by birth and later became part of the Weasley family through marriage. Hermione Granger was a human female of English nationality. Hermione Granger was known by the aliases "Hermy," a nickname used by Grawp, and "Grant," a name used by Professor Cuthbert Binns. During undercover operations, Hermione Granger also assumed the identities of Harry Potter, Mafalda Hopkirk, Bellatrix Lestrange, and the false identity Penelope Clearwater through disguise or deception.

Hermione Granger discovered that she was a witch at the age of eleven after spending her childhood believing herself to be an ordinary Muggle. Hermione Granger received admission to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in 1991. Hermione Granger was Sorted into Gryffindor House, although the Sorting Hat considered placing her in Ravenclaw because of her extraordinary intelligence and academic ability. Hermione Granger rapidly established herself as one of the most gifted students of her generation and consistently achieved the highest academic standards across nearly every magical subject.

Before attending Hogwarts, Hermione Granger practised simple spells independently, and every spell she attempted reportedly worked successfully. This early magical success demonstrated Hermione Granger's exceptional natural aptitude for magic long before receiving formal magical education.

Hermione Granger first met Harry Potter and Ronald "Ron" Weasley aboard the Hogwarts Express in 1991. Harry Potter and Ron Weasley initially considered Hermione Granger to be unfriendly, overly eager to answer questions, and an insufferable know-it-all because of her constant displays of knowledge and her desire to impress professors. Their relationship changed dramatically during the Hallowe'en troll incident of 1991. After Harry Potter and Ron Weasley rescued Hermione Granger from a mountain troll inside Hogwarts Castle, Hermione Granger deliberately accepted responsibility for the incident to protect them from punishment. Hermione Granger's willingness to sacrifice her own reputation for Harry Potter and Ron Weasley created mutual trust, leading the three students to become inseparable friends known collectively as the Trio.

Throughout Hermione Granger's life at Hogwarts, Hermione Granger remained one of Harry Potter's closest companions and strongest supporters. Hermione Granger repeatedly provided Harry Potter with intellectual guidance, practical magical assistance, strategic planning, and emotional encouragement. Hermione Granger believed that friendship and bravery were more important than intelligence alone, despite recognising Harry Potter's extraordinary magical abilities. Harry Potter consistently regarded Hermione Granger as the most intelligent student in their year and frequently relied upon her knowledge to solve difficult magical problems.

During the 1991–1992 school year, Hermione Granger played an essential role in protecting the Philosopher's Stone from Lord Voldemort. Hermione Granger's intelligence, magical knowledge, and logical reasoning enabled the Trio to overcome several magical obstacles guarding the Stone. Hermione Granger's contribution directly helped prevent Lord Voldemort from obtaining immortality through the Philosopher's Stone.

During her second year at Hogwarts in 1992–1993, Hermione Granger became deeply involved in investigating the mystery surrounding the Chamber of Secrets. Hermione Granger successfully deduced that the monster attacking students was a Basilisk. Before Hermione Granger could communicate her discovery directly, the Basilisk petrified her. Hermione Granger recovered after Madam Pomfrey administered Professor Sprout's Mandrake Restorative Draught. Hermione Granger's earlier deduction provided crucial evidence that enabled the Chamber of Secrets to be solved.

In her second year, Hermione Granger successfully brewed the highly advanced Polyjuice Potion despite being only twelve years old. Hermione Granger, Harry Potter, and Ron Weasley used the potion to disguise themselves as Draco Malfoy's classmates in order to investigate Draco Malfoy's possible connection to the Chamber of Secrets. Brewing the potion demonstrated Hermione Granger's extraordinary talent in Potions far beyond the expected level of a second-year student.

Before beginning her third year in 1993, Hermione Granger received official permission from the British Ministry of Magic to possess and use a Time-Turner. Hermione Granger received the Time-Turner because she wished to attend more classes than could fit into a normal school timetable. Hermione Granger used the magical artifact responsibly throughout the school year to study numerous advanced subjects simultaneously. At the conclusion of the school year, Hermione Granger and Harry Potter used the Time-Turner to rescue Sirius Black from receiving the Dementor's Kiss and to save Buckbeak the hippogriff from execution. Hermione Granger later returned the Time-Turner to the Ministry of Magic and resumed a normal academic schedule.

During her fourth year in 1994–1995, Hermione Granger became a leading advocate for house-elf rights. Hermione Granger founded the Society for the Promotion of Elfish Welfare, commonly abbreviated as S.P.E.W. Hermione Granger believed that house-elves deserved freedom, wages, and equal treatment. Hermione Granger's campaign reflected her strong moral commitment to justice, even though many witches, wizards, and house-elves themselves disagreed with her interpretation of their circumstances. Hermione Granger continued supporting improved treatment of magical creatures after leaving Hogwarts, making this belief a lifelong commitment rather than a temporary school project.

During the Triwizard Tournament, Hermione Granger provided Harry Potter with substantial assistance while Harry Potter competed unwillingly in the competition. Hermione Granger researched magical information, developed strategies, and offered emotional support throughout the tournament.

During the Yule Ball in 1994, Hermione Granger underwent a dramatic physical transformation by using Sleekeazy's Hair Potion to tame her bushy hair into a sleek and elegant hairstyle. Hermione Granger wore elegant periwinkle-blue robes and surprised many students, including Harry Potter, Pansy Parkinson, and Draco Malfoy, who had rarely seen Hermione Granger place attention on her appearance.

During her fifth year in 1995–1996, Hermione Granger became a Gryffindor Prefect because of her responsibility, discipline, and leadership. Hermione Granger was the principal architect behind the creation of Dumbledore's Army after the Ministry of Magic interfered with practical Defence Against the Dark Arts instruction at Hogwarts. Hermione Granger organised the secret student resistance movement so that students could continue learning defensive magic under Harry Potter's instruction. Hermione Granger also secretly enchanted the membership list so that any member who betrayed Dumbledore's Army would develop permanent facial boils spelling the word "SNEAK." This defensive measure later exposed Marietta Edgecombe after the betrayal of the organisation.

Hermione Granger fought alongside Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and fellow members of Dumbledore's Army during the Battle of the Department of Mysteries. Hermione Granger's participation demonstrated that she had evolved from an academically focused student into an experienced combatant willing to risk her life against Death Eaters.

During her sixth year in 1996–1997, Hermione Granger continued serving as a Prefect while remaining one of Harry Potter's closest advisers. Hermione Granger opposed Harry Potter's reliance on the annotated Half-Blood Prince's Potions textbook because Hermione Granger distrusted unknown magical sources without proper verification. Hermione Granger's disagreement with Harry Potter illustrated both her commitment to caution and the occasional limitations of her rigid reliance on evidence and established authority. Hermione Granger also participated in the Battle of the Astronomy Tower alongside members of the Order of the Phoenix and Hogwarts defenders.

In 1997, Hermione Granger fought in the Battle of the Seven Potters together with the Order of the Phoenix while helping protect Harry Potter from Lord Voldemort and the Death Eaters. Following the fall of the Ministry of Magic, Hermione Granger chose not to return to Hogwarts for her seventh year. Instead, Hermione Granger joined Harry Potter and Ron Weasley on the Horcrux Hunt because defeating Lord Voldemort became more important than completing formal education.

During the Horcrux Hunt, Hermione Granger repeatedly preserved the survival of the Trio through planning, magical preparation, protective enchantments, and advanced spellcasting. Hermione Granger demonstrated remarkable courage despite constant pursuit by Death Eaters and the Ministry controlled by Lord Voldemort. Hermione Granger was captured by Snatchers together with Harry Potter and Ron Weasley. Bellatrix Lestrange personally tortured Hermione Granger, but Hermione Granger refused to surrender vital information despite extreme physical suffering. Hermione Granger's endurance demonstrated exceptional mental resilience and loyalty.

Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley entered the Chamber of Secrets during the Horcrux Hunt and retrieved Basilisk fangs. Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley used a Basilisk fang to destroy Hufflepuff's Cup, one of Lord Voldemort's Horcruxes. Destroying the Horcrux permanently weakened Lord Voldemort by eliminating one fragment of his divided soul. Hermione Granger's direct participation in destroying a Horcrux made her one of the individuals most responsible for Lord Voldemort's eventual defeat.

During the Battle of Hogwarts in 1998, Hermione Granger fought alongside Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, the Order of the Phoenix, Dumbledore's Army, Hogwarts staff, and numerous defenders against Lord Voldemort and the Death Eaters. Hermione Granger helped defeat Bellatrix Lestrange, the Death Eater responsible for torturing Hermione during the Horcrux Hunt. Hermione Granger consistently demonstrated quick reflexes, effective duelling ability, and leadership during combat.

Following the end of the Second Wizarding War, Hermione Granger returned to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry to complete her formal magical education. Hermione Granger later accepted employment in the Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures within the British Ministry of Magic. Hermione Granger used this position to continue advocating for improved treatment and legal protection of house-elves and other magical beings. Hermione Granger was later promoted to the Department of Magical Law Enforcement, eventually becoming Deputy Head of the department. Hermione Granger worked to eliminate discriminatory laws favouring pure-blood witches and wizards, demonstrating continuity between her school-age activism and her adult political career.

By the year 2019, Hermione Granger became the thirty-sixth Minister for Magic. Hermione Granger also held the title of Senior Minister. As Minister for Magic, Hermione Granger represented the highest political authority within magical Britain. Hermione Granger's appointment reflected widespread recognition of her intelligence, integrity, wartime service, and commitment to legal reform.

Hermione Granger eventually married Ronald Weasley. Their friendship gradually developed into a romantic relationship after years of shared danger, personal growth, disagreements, reconciliation, and mutual loyalty. Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley became the parents of Rose Granger-Weasley and Hugo Granger-Weasley. Through marriage, Hermione Granger became the daughter-in-law of Arthur Weasley and Molly Weasley, the sister-in-law of Bill Weasley, Charlie Weasley, Percy Weasley, Fred Weasley, George Weasley, Ginny Potter, Fleur Weasley, Audrey Weasley, and Angelina Weasley, and the aunt by marriage of numerous members of the extended Weasley and Potter families. Hermione Granger also became the godmother of James Sirius Potter, the eldest son of Harry Potter and Ginny Potter, demonstrating the lifelong bond between Hermione Granger and Harry Potter.

Before marrying Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger briefly developed a romantic connection with Viktor Krum during the Triwizard Tournament. Hermione Granger also attended a date with Cormac McLaggen, although the relationship did not continue.

Hermione Granger's Boggart assumed the form of academic failure. When confronted with the Boggart, Hermione Granger imagined Professor Minerva McGonagall announcing that Hermione Granger had failed every examination. This fear revealed Hermione Granger's deep insecurity regarding personal failure despite her extraordinary academic achievements.

Hermione Granger possessed light skin, bright brown eyes, and long bushy brown hair frequently described as resembling a mane. Hermione Granger had noticeably large front teeth during childhood. After accidentally being struck by a Densaugeo spell in 1994, Madam Pomfrey magically reduced Hermione Granger's teeth. Hermione Granger intentionally allowed the magical shrinking process to continue beyond its original point so that her teeth became evenly aligned, avoiding the braces originally planned by her dentist parents. Hermione Granger later explained that she had previously avoided magical cosmetic correction out of respect for her parents.

Hermione Granger usually wore simple black robes and casual clothing. Hermione Granger generally paid little attention to physical appearance, preferring books and study over fashion. Hermione Granger often carried large numbers of books. Hermione Granger's appearance reflected her priorities, although special occasions such as the Yule Ball and Bill Weasley and Fleur Delacour's wedding demonstrated that Hermione Granger could appear highly elegant when she deliberately chose to do so.

Hermione Granger possessed an exceptional work ethic, extraordinary curiosity, and remarkable self-discipline. Hermione Granger read extensively for recreation as well as academic study. Hermione Granger frequently visited the Hogwarts library whenever difficult questions arose. Ron Weasley famously characterised Hermione Granger by observing that Hermione Granger always went to the library whenever uncertainty appeared.

Hermione Granger consistently relied upon logical reasoning alongside magical knowledge. Hermione Granger successfully deduced that Remus Lupin was a werewolf because of logical analysis rather than intuition. Hermione Granger often required evidence before accepting extraordinary claims. This scepticism caused Hermione Granger initially to reject the existence or importance of the Deathly Hallows because Hermione Granger demanded empirical proof. Hermione Granger's commitment to logic occasionally prevented Hermione Granger from recognising possibilities that lacked immediate evidence.

Hermione Granger displayed strong compassion and a powerful sense of justice. Hermione Granger devoted substantial time to defending Buckbeak against execution and campaigning for house-elf rights. Hermione Granger's compassion motivated many of her major life decisions and eventually shaped her Ministry career.

Hermione Granger could be blunt, argumentative, bossy, and tactless. Hermione Granger sometimes struggled to comfort others emotionally despite sincerely caring about them. Hermione Granger attempted to advise Harry Potter regarding Cho Chang's behaviour and helped Ginny Weasley understand her feelings for Harry Potter. Hermione Granger often provided practical advice to friends, demonstrating emotional intelligence even though social diplomacy was not always Hermione Granger's strongest skill.

Hermione Granger valued rules, responsibility, and order. Hermione Granger frequently acted as the voice of reason among more impulsive companions. Hermione Granger sometimes enforced rules even when doing so created conflict with Harry Potter or Ron Weasley. Hermione Granger persuaded authorities to confiscate Harry Potter's Firebolt because Hermione Granger feared the broomstick might be cursed. Hermione Granger also strongly opposed Harry Potter's continued use of the Half-Blood Prince's textbook because Hermione Granger prioritised safety over convenience.

Although Hermione Granger generally respected rules, Hermione Granger willingly violated laws or ethical conventions when doing so protected innocent people or advanced a greater moral objective. Hermione Granger blackmailed Rita Skeeter into publishing favourable information about Harry Potter. Hermione Granger slapped Draco Malfoy after Draco Malfoy insulted Rubeus Hagrid. Hermione Granger cast a Confundus Charm upon Cormac McLaggen after Cormac McLaggen insulted Hermione Granger's friends. Hermione Granger magically attacked Ron Weasley with conjured canaries after feeling deeply hurt by Ron Weasley's relationship with Lavender Brown. These incidents demonstrated that Hermione Granger combined moral conviction with fierce protectiveness toward the people Hermione Granger loved.

Hermione Granger openly embraced her Muggle-born identity despite repeated discrimination. Hermione Granger proudly identified herself as a "Mudblood" when speaking with Griphook, refusing to allow a hateful slur to diminish her identity. Hermione Granger consistently rejected pure-blood prejudice and later dedicated her political career to dismantling discriminatory legislation.

Hermione Granger gradually overcame an early tendency toward panic during dangerous situations. Through repeated exposure to life-threatening events, Hermione Granger became increasingly calm, decisive, courageous, and resourceful. Hermione Granger repeatedly risked death to protect Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and the wider wizarding community. Hermione Granger's personal development transformed Hermione Granger from an academically brilliant but socially awkward first-year student into one of the central leaders of the resistance against Lord Voldemort and ultimately into Minister for Magic.

Remus Lupin described Hermione Granger as the cleverest witch of her age. Rubeus Hagrid declared that no spell had been invented that Hermione Granger could not perform. Harry Potter repeatedly described Hermione Granger as the best student in their year. Horace Slughorn praised Hermione Granger's encyclopedic magical knowledge and awarded Gryffindor House twenty points after witnessing Hermione Granger's exceptional understanding.

Hermione Granger excelled in Charms, Transfiguration, Potions, Herbology, Arithmancy, Defence Against the Dark Arts, Alchemy, and numerous other magical disciplines. Hermione Granger mastered spells faster than nearly every classmate and regularly performed magic beyond her educational level. Hermione Granger achieved ten O.W.L.s, receiving nine Outstanding grades and one Exceeds Expectations in Defence Against the Dark Arts. Hermione Granger's only consistently difficult spell was the Patronus Charm, highlighting the exceptional standard of Hermione Granger's magical abilities.

Hermione Granger demonstrated exceptional talent with Charms by mastering the Levitation Charm, Unlocking Charm, Flashing Paint Charm, Shield Charm, Memory Charm, and many advanced protective enchantments. Hermione Granger possessed outstanding Transfiguration ability and was the first student in her class to transform a match into a needle successfully. Hermione Granger became highly proficient in Potions, successfully brewing Polyjuice Potion during second year. Hermione Granger possessed advanced understanding of Alchemy, Golpalott's Third Law, Herbology, and magical theory. Hermione Granger also displayed considerable combat skill through defensive and offensive spellcasting, including the Full Body-Bind Curse, Leg-Locker Curse, Jelly-Legs Jinx, Oppugno, Stinging Hex, Blasting Curse, and sophisticated protective enchantments.

Hermione Granger was a capable flyer despite lacking confidence on a broomstick. Hermione Granger successfully flew through the Underground Chambers during the protection of the Philosopher's Stone to retrieve a Winged Key.

Hermione Granger's Patronus took the corporeal form of an otter. Hermione Granger's wand measured ten and three-quarter inches, consisted of vine wood with a dragon heartstring core, and served as Hermione Granger's primary wand until its confiscation by Snatchers in 1998. After losing the original wand, Hermione Granger temporarily wielded Bellatrix Lestrange's walnut wand with a dragon heartstring core.

Hermione Granger owned a Time-Turner during her third year, a Revealer capable of exposing invisible ink, numerous spellbooks covering many magical disciplines, and an extensive library of Dark Arts reference books. Although Hermione Granger never practised as a Dark witch, Hermione Granger studied dark magic academically in order to understand dangerous magical phenomena such as Horcruxes. During the Horcrux Hunt, Hermione Granger carried the book Secrets of the Darkest Art, using its information to understand the creation and destruction of Horcruxes.

Hermione Granger's enduring legacy is defined by extraordinary intelligence, unwavering loyalty, moral courage, dedication to equality, exceptional magical ability, and transformative public service. Hermione Granger profoundly influenced Harry Potter's success, contributed directly to Lord Voldemort's defeat, advanced civil rights for magical beings, reformed discriminatory magical law, and ultimately became one of the most influential political leaders in modern wizarding history.
