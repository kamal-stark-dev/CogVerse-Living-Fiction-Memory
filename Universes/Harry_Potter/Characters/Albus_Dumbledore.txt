Albus Percival Wulfric Brian Dumbledore was an English half-blood wizard who lived between August 1881 and 30 June 1997. He was born in Mould-on-the-Wold, England, and died at the Astronomy Tower of Hogwarts Castle in the Highlands of Scotland at the age of 115. Throughout his lifetime, Albus Dumbledore became widely regarded as the greatest wizard of his era and by many as the greatest wizard in history. He served as Professor of Defence Against the Dark Arts, Professor of Transfiguration, Head of the Transfiguration Department, and ultimately Headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Outside Hogwarts, he served as Supreme Mugwump of the International Confederation of Wizards until 1995 and as Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot until 1995 before later being reinstated in 1996 and serving until his death in 1997. He received numerous honours, including the Order of Merlin, First Class, and the title of Grand Sorcerer. Although remembered throughout most of his life as a defender of Muggle rights and equality, his beliefs during his youth were dramatically different before he experienced profound personal tragedy and moral transformation.

Albus Dumbledore was the eldest child of Percival Dumbledore and Kendra Dumbledore. He had a younger brother, Aberforth Dumbledore, and a younger sister, Ariana Dumbledore. His extended family included his aunt Honoria and the wider Dumbledore family. Aurelius Dumbledore, also known as Credence Barebone, was regarded as his biological nephew according to the provided information. Albus Dumbledore never married. He experienced a romantic relationship with Gellert Grindelwald during his youth, but the relationship ended after ideological conflict and personal tragedy. Despite never entering another romantic relationship, Grindelwald remained one of the defining influences on Dumbledore's life.

Albus Dumbledore belonged to Gryffindor House during his education at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. During his student years, he became a Prefect and later Head Boy. Even as a child and teenager, he displayed extraordinary magical talent and intellectual brilliance. Professors and fellow students regarded him as one of the most gifted students ever educated at Hogwarts. He accumulated numerous awards and honours that reflected exceptional magical ability far beyond that expected of a student. Elphias Doge later described him as the most gifted student Hogwarts had ever produced, while others considered Tom Riddle to be the only student who might rival Dumbledore's natural ability.

Albus Dumbledore's early life was permanently shaped by tragedy. His father, Percival Dumbledore, died while imprisoned in Azkaban during Albus's youth. Later, his mother, Kendra Dumbledore, died, leaving Albus responsible for caring for Aberforth and Ariana. Ariana Dumbledore later died during a three-way duel involving Albus Dumbledore, Aberforth Dumbledore, and Gellert Grindelwald. Because the exact spell that caused Ariana's death was never determined, Albus Dumbledore spent the remainder of his life believing that he might have been responsible. This uncertainty became the defining source of guilt throughout his life. His Boggart assumed the form of Ariana's corpse, demonstrating that her death remained his deepest fear and greatest emotional wound.

During his youth, Albus Dumbledore fell deeply in love with Gellert Grindelwald. Together they developed the revolutionary philosophy known as "For the Greater Good." Both young wizards believed that exceptionally powerful wizards should rule over Muggles for what they considered the benefit of both magical and non-magical society. Their shared ambitions included reorganising wizarding society and establishing magical dominance. This period represented the greatest moral failure of Dumbledore's life. Ariana Dumbledore's death shattered both the relationship and Dumbledore's political beliefs. From that point onward, Albus Dumbledore rejected wizard supremacy, abandoned Grindelwald's ideology, and dedicated the rest of his life to protecting both witches, wizards, and Muggles from tyranny.

Although Dumbledore opposed Grindelwald after Ariana's death, several factors prevented him from confronting Grindelwald directly for many years. Albus Dumbledore and Gellert Grindelwald had entered into a magical blood pact that prevented either wizard from acting directly against the other. In addition, Dumbledore remained haunted by his love for Grindelwald, his guilt regarding Ariana's death, and concern that Grindelwald might know the truth about what happened during Ariana's final moments. These emotional and magical barriers delayed the inevitable confrontation.

Recognising the growing danger posed by Grindelwald, Albus Dumbledore instead chose indirect resistance. He sent Newt Scamander to New York to release the Thunderbird Frank. Later, Dumbledore sent Newt Scamander to Paris to rescue Credence Barebone. Following Leta Lestrange's death in Paris, Dumbledore became increasingly committed to supporting resistance against Grindelwald. He provided strategic guidance and encouragement to Newt Scamander, Theseus Scamander, Tina Goldstein, Jacob Kowalski, Yusuf Kama, and Nagini throughout the Global Wizarding War. During this conflict, Grindelwald secretly intended to manipulate Aurelius Dumbledore, also known as Credence Barebone, into becoming a weapon against Albus Dumbledore because of his alleged relationship to the Dumbledore family.

After the conclusion of the Global Wizarding War, Albus Dumbledore finally confronted Gellert Grindelwald in what later became celebrated as one of the greatest duels in magical history. Dumbledore defeated Grindelwald and ended his reign of terror. This victory established Dumbledore's worldwide reputation as the greatest living wizard. Following Grindelwald's defeat, Dumbledore became the rightful master of the Elder Wand, one of the three Deathly Hallows, and retained mastery of the wand from 1945 until 1997.

Alongside his military and political achievements, Albus Dumbledore made lasting scholarly contributions to magical knowledge. He collaborated with Nicolas Flamel, the only known creator of the Philosopher's Stone, in advanced alchemical research. He became internationally recognised for discovering the twelve uses of dragon's blood. He also contributed articles to scholarly publications including *Transfiguration Today*, *Challenges in Charming*, and *The Practical Potioneer*. These accomplishments established him not only as a powerful wizard but also as one of the greatest magical scholars of his age.

Albus Dumbledore devoted much of his life to education. He reportedly wished to become a teacher from an early age. He first served as Professor of Defence Against the Dark Arts at Hogwarts before later becoming Professor of Transfiguration. He subsequently became Head of the Transfiguration Department before eventually being appointed Headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. His teaching combined extraordinary magical knowledge with exceptional emotional intelligence. He became widely regarded as one of the greatest professors in Hogwarts history and ultimately one of its most beloved Headmasters.

As Headmaster, Albus Dumbledore consistently believed that every individual deserved the opportunity for redemption. He strongly opposed the expulsion of Newton Scamander from Hogwarts. He later protected Rubeus Hagrid after Hagrid was falsely blamed for opening the Chamber of Secrets. Dumbledore admitted Remus Lupin as a student despite Lupin being a werewolf and later appointed him as Defence Against the Dark Arts professor despite widespread prejudice. Dumbledore remained one of Hagrid's closest friends despite society's distrust of half-giants. He also refused to expel Jacob's sibling despite repeated disciplinary problems involving the Cursed Vaults.

Albus Dumbledore demonstrated remarkable faith in Severus Snape. After Snape expressed genuine remorse over his actions that contributed to Lily Evans Potter's death, Dumbledore accepted him as an ally and entrusted him with critical responsibilities. Dumbledore deliberately relied upon Snape's enduring love for Lily Evans to secure Snape's loyalty and commitment to protecting Harry Potter. This relationship illustrated both Dumbledore's willingness to believe in redemption and his readiness to manipulate circumstances when he believed the greater good required it.

Even before Tom Riddle became Lord Voldemort, Albus Dumbledore recognised dangerous characteristics within the boy. Unlike almost everyone else, Dumbledore remained immune to Tom Riddle's charm and manipulation. Although Dumbledore hoped Tom Riddle might choose a better path, he carefully monitored the student throughout his Hogwarts education because he recognised his cruelty, ambition, and sociopathic tendencies. These early observations later became essential in understanding Voldemort's psychology and eventually discovering the locations of Voldemort's Horcruxes.

Albus Dumbledore became the central organiser of resistance against Lord Voldemort. During the First Wizarding War, he founded and led the Order of the Phoenix to oppose Voldemort and the Death Eaters. Following Voldemort's return and Cornelius Fudge's refusal to acknowledge the threat, Dumbledore lost his political offices after conflict with the Ministry of Magic. When Voldemort's return became undeniable, Dumbledore was reinstated as Chief Warlock and Supreme Mugwump. At the beginning of the Second Wizarding War, Dumbledore re-established the Order of the Phoenix and again assumed leadership of the resistance against Voldemort.

Throughout Harry Potter's education, Albus Dumbledore acted as Harry Potter's mentor, protector, and strategic guide. He consistently assisted Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger throughout their first six years at Hogwarts. He also supported Jacob's sibling during the search for the Cursed Vaults. The student resistance organisation known as Dumbledore's Army was named in his honour because students regarded Dumbledore as the symbol of resistance against tyranny.

Lord Voldemort regarded Albus Dumbledore as his greatest enemy and the only wizard he genuinely feared. Dumbledore's immense magical ability, strategic intelligence, leadership, and moral authority placed him beyond Voldemort's confidence. Harry Potter himself repeatedly experienced complete confidence whenever Dumbledore arrived during dangerous situations, recognising that Dumbledore's presence often shifted seemingly hopeless battles in favour of the defenders.

Albus Dumbledore possessed extraordinary magical ability across nearly every known branch of magic. His magical talent was evident from childhood and continued developing throughout his life. He excelled in Transfiguration, Charms, Defence Against the Dark Arts, and duelling. Examiners noted that he performed magical feats during his N.E.W.T. examinations unlike anything they had previously witnessed. His magical knowledge extended into alchemy, magical theory, leadership, teaching, and psychological understanding. He possessed exceptional charisma and emotional intelligence, enabling him both to inspire loyalty and, when necessary, manipulate others toward outcomes he considered morally necessary.

Albus Dumbledore's skill as a duellist became legendary. Even during his youth he successfully participated in the three-way duel involving Aberforth Dumbledore and Gellert Grindelwald. His later defeat of Grindelwald became one of the most famous magical duels ever recorded. During his old age, he remained powerful enough that even Lord Voldemort struggled against him. His rare displays of anger transformed his normally gentle appearance into one of overwhelming magical intimidation. Harry Potter observed that these moments explained why Voldemort feared Dumbledore above all others.

Albus Dumbledore's Patronus took the form of a phoenix. His Boggart manifested as Ariana Dumbledore's corpse. During most of his life he wielded an earlier wand of unknown wood and core before becoming master of the Elder Wand, a fifteen-inch wand made from elder wood with a Thestral tail hair core. The Elder Wand remained loyal to Dumbledore until Draco Malfoy disarmed him atop the Astronomy Tower. The wand's allegiance later passed indirectly to Harry Potter. Dumbledore deliberately concealed the Elder Wand's identity and refused to exploit its immense power for cruelty or personal ambition.

Albus Dumbledore designed and owned the Deluminator, a magical device capable of removing and restoring light while also functioning as a means of guiding its owner to loved ones. After Dumbledore's death, the Deluminator became part of his carefully arranged legacy by being inherited by Ron Weasley. Dumbledore also owned a Pensieve, which he regularly used to organise memories, examine complicated events, and identify hidden connections. He stored both his own memories and those of others within the Pensieve. Harry Potter later used the Pensieve to study Igor Karkaroff's trial, Severus Snape's memories, and numerous memories concerning Tom Riddle's past.

Albus Dumbledore owned a phoenix named Fawkes. Fawkes remained Dumbledore's loyal companion throughout much of his life and departed Hogwarts following Dumbledore's death. Two feathers from Fawkes became the cores of the wands belonging to Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort, creating an important magical connection between those two rivals. Phoenixes were considered extremely difficult to domesticate, making Dumbledore's relationship with Fawkes another testament to his extraordinary magical affinity. According to family tradition, phoenixes possessed a special connection with members of the Dumbledore family and would appear to them during moments of great need.

Albus Dumbledore also possessed a diary in which he regularly wrote and carried a distinctive pocket watch featuring twelve hands with miniature planets replacing conventional numbers. His office contained numerous unusual magical instruments reflecting his constant pursuit of magical research and understanding.

Physically, Albus Dumbledore stood approximately five feet eleven inches tall. During youth he possessed auburn hair, while in old age both his hair and beard became long and silver, extending far enough to tuck into his belt. He had fair skin, brilliant blue eyes, and a very long crooked nose believed to have been broken multiple times, possibly including a punch delivered by Aberforth Dumbledore during Ariana's funeral. He usually wore half-moon spectacles together with colourful robes, purple cloaks, crimson garments, and high-heeled buckled boots. His long fingers were noted for their remarkable dexterity. During the final year of his life, Marvolo Gaunt's cursed ring permanently blackened and withered his right hand despite Severus Snape successfully slowing the curse's progress.

The curse contained within Marvolo Gaunt's Ring fundamentally altered the final stage of Albus Dumbledore's life. After placing the ring upon his finger, Dumbledore suffered a deadly magical curse that Severus Snape managed only to contain temporarily. Snape informed Dumbledore that the curse would inevitably spread and kill him within approximately one year. Knowing his death had become unavoidable, Dumbledore carefully transformed his inevitable death into a strategic advantage against Voldemort.

To protect Draco Malfoy from becoming a murderer and to preserve Severus Snape's position as Voldemort's trusted agent, Albus Dumbledore arranged for Severus Snape to kill him at the appropriate moment. During the Battle of the Astronomy Tower on 30 June 1997, Severus Snape fulfilled this agreement by casting the fatal curse that killed Dumbledore. Although Dumbledore died at Snape's hand, his death occurred according to a mutually agreed plan intended to strengthen the long-term resistance against Voldemort.

Albus Dumbledore believed that death represented "the next great adventure" for an organised mind. He therefore faced his own death without fear. Following his death, Harry Potter encountered Dumbledore's spirit in Limbo, where Dumbledore appeared healthy, upright, and restored, with his previously cursed hand completely healed. Even in death, Dumbledore remained curious about what lay beyond mortal existence.

Although Albus Dumbledore died before the conclusion of the Second Wizarding War, his carefully prepared plans continued shaping events after his death. Through his accumulated knowledge, strategic planning, manipulation of Voldemort's assumptions, trust in Harry Potter, and carefully arranged succession of responsibilities, Dumbledore ensured that Voldemort's Horcruxes could be destroyed and that Harry Potter would ultimately defeat Lord Voldemort. His long-term planning directly contributed to the permanent restoration of peace within the wizarding world.

Albus Dumbledore became the only Headmaster of Hogwarts to be buried on Hogwarts grounds. Following his death, a magical portrait of him was placed within the Headmaster's office so that future Headmasters could continue seeking his guidance. Harry Potter later honoured Dumbledore's memory by naming his second son Albus Severus Potter after both Albus Dumbledore and Severus Snape.

Albus Dumbledore's legacy combined extraordinary magical achievement with profound moral complexity. His early embrace of wizard supremacy evolved into lifelong opposition to tyranny after personal tragedy transformed his worldview. His scholarship advanced magical knowledge through alchemy, dragon's blood research, and education. His leadership shaped resistance against both Gellert Grindelwald and Lord Voldemort. His mentorship influenced generations of students, including Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, Rubeus Hagrid, Newton Scamander, Remus Lupin, Severus Snape, and many others. His willingness to grant second chances redeemed individuals who might otherwise have become enemies. His strategic foresight continued influencing events after his death, making him one of the most consequential figures in the history of the modern wizarding world.
